## FAQ Block — Verbatim Copy

```
Q1: What's a private sunset yacht charter?
A1: The entire 47-foot Malarky catamaran for just your group, timed around sunset. You and up to 11 guests. Captain and crew handle the boat. The route is yours — usually we'll position for the best angle on the Coronado Bridge and downtown skyline at golden hour. No shared deck, no other guests, no ticketed strangers. The opposite of a ticketed cruise.

Q2: How is this different from a ticketed sunset cruise?
A2: Ticketed sunset cruises sell individual seats — you're on a larger boat (60 to 100 guests) with people who bought their own tickets. They're cheaper per person and they're a great option if that's what you want — Triton Charters and Athena Boat Rental both run ticketed sunset cruises with full bars for around $55 per person. Malarky is the private alternative: more expensive in total, but the whole boat is yours. Different products for different occasions.

Q3: What time does the charter depart?
A3: Depends on the season. Sunset shifts from around 4:45pm in December to nearly 8:00pm in late June. We aim to depart 2 to 2.5 hours before sunset so you're positioned at the best stretch of bay as light peaks. We'll send the exact departure time with your quote based on your date.

Q4: How long is a typical private sunset charter?
A4: 2.5 to 3 hours is the sweet spot. That gives time to get underway, settle in with drinks, catch the full color show before, during, and after sunset, and cruise back during the blue-hour quiet afterward. Shorter feels rushed; longer is fine if your group wants more time.

Q5: Can I bring my own drinks for a sunset charter?
A5: Yes — BYOB is allowed and encouraged. A bottle of champagne for the sunset moment, a few bottles of wine, a cooler of beer, premium spirits — bring whatever you like. Two rules: no glass containers (we transfer to plastic before boarding) and no red wine (it permanently stains the deck). Most sunset charters lean toward champagne and wine over heavy cocktails.

Q6: Is this good for a proposal or anniversary?
A6: It's one of the most-requested occasions on Malarky for a reason. The bow trampoline at golden hour with the Coronado Bridge behind you is genuinely cinematic. If you're planning to propose, tell us when you book and we'll coordinate timing so the moment lands right (champagne pre-positioned, captain holds course for the photo, crew gives you space). See our proposal yacht charter and anniversary charter pages for occasion-specific details.

Q7: Do you do private sunset charters year-round?
A7: Yes. Sunset is beautiful on San Diego Bay every month — the colors change with the season (clearer skies in winter, hazier warmer light in summer), and the departure time shifts accordingly. Winter evenings cool off faster, so layers and the indoor lounge come into play more. Summer evenings are warm well past sunset. We charter through the calendar.
```

## FAQ Block — Verbatim Copy

```
Q1: How do we coordinate the actual moment with the captain?
A1: We schedule a pre-charter call about a week before your booking to walk through the plan. You tell us the moment you want (bow trampoline at golden hour is most common), how you want to signal the captain that you're ready, where the photographer will be, and any other details — surprise level, family aboard or not, champagne reveal, anything specific. On the day, the captain reads the situation, positions the boat at peak light, eases the engine so the moment isn't drowned by motor noise, and gives you space until you cue otherwise. We've done this many times; we don't ad-lib.

Q2: Can we have a photographer aboard?
A2: Yes — three options. (a) Bring your own professional photographer — they board as one of your guests, position discreetly, capture from the aft deck or upper helm. (b) Have a friend capture it on a phone — works for casual styles. (c) We can recommend local San Diego proposal photographers who've shot many Malarky proposals; they know the bow angles, the lighting timing, and our crew. Drones are allowed in San Diego Bay with proper permits — ask if you're considering one.

Q3: Can we surprise our partner — make them think it's just a sunset cruise?
A3: Yes, this is the most common framing. You book what your partner believes is a "sunset cruise for our anniversary" or a "birthday treat" or "I thought it'd be fun." They have no idea what's planned. The crew doesn't acknowledge it's a proposal in front of them. We've kept hundreds of secrets at this point.

Q4: What if friends or family want to be part of the moment?
A4: Two common approaches. (a) Friends hidden in the indoor lounge before your partner arrives; they emerge for the celebration after the yes. We coordinate the timing. (b) Family already aboard as part of a "family yacht day" — your partner suspects something is up but doesn't know what specifically. Captain reads the situation and cues the moment when ready.

Q5: What if the weather or timing doesn't cooperate on the day?
A5: Two flexes. (a) If conditions threaten the bridge backdrop (fog, wind), the captain proposes an alternative location with similar visual impact — back-bay anchorage, downtown-skyline angle, or a sheltered cove. (b) If the moment runs long and golden hour passes, the captain extends so the photo still has a great backdrop — blue-hour downtown skyline is the second-best option. We won't let the moment happen against a flat-gray backdrop just because the clock said so.

Q6: Is BYOB really allowed on a proposal charter?
A6: Yes — and it's actually the right move. Bring premium champagne (Veuve, Dom, whatever marks the moment) at retail prices instead of marked up 3-4x on a bar tab. We provide a cooler with ice. Two rules: no glass (we transfer to plastic before boarding) and no red wine. Champagne, sparkling, white wine, and spirits are all welcome.

Q7: What does it cost?
A7: Proposal charters are quote-based and depend on duration, day of week, and time of year. A typical 2.5 to 3-hour evening proposal charter is the standard. Call (844) 724-5787 or send the booking request and we'll send a quote with your date options. Cancellation: full deposit refund 3+ weeks before, free reschedule up to 1 week prior, weather-related flexibility on shorter notice.
```

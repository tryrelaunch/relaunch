## Why this rename specifically

The current `/corporate-yacht-charter-san-diego/` page targets a head term Malarky structurally loses on:

- "Corporate yacht charter" intent is *team event* / *all-hands* / *holiday party* / *sales kickoff* — typical group size 30–80, sometimes 100+.
- Malarky's hard cap is 12. The page can't compete for the bulk of corporate-charter intent because the boat doesn't physically scale to it.
- Triton (100-cap, bartender, dance floor) is the right fleet boat for that intent and ranks/converts accordingly.

Meanwhile, there's a *distinct* high-value bottom-of-funnel intent Malarky structurally wins on:

- C-suite offsites, board retreats, leadership meetings (typically 6–12 people)
- Client entertainment dinners (typically 4–10 attendees including spouses/partners)
- Executive milestone events (promotion celebrations, partner welcomes, deal-closing dinners)
- All of these explicitly prefer *small group, private, premium, no other guests* — Malarky's defining positioning.

The keyword shift is: away from "corporate" (Triton's lane) toward "executive" (Malarky's defensible lane). The page repositions accordingly.

## FAQ Block — Verbatim Copy

```
Q1: What makes Malarky right for executive charters specifically?
A1: Twelve-guest cap, private at all times (no shared boarding, no other groups), USCG-certified captain, indoor lounge for breakout conversations, discretion-trained crew, full BYO/catering flexibility, and a 47-foot Voyage catamaran that doesn't look or feel like a generic tour boat. It's the difference between "we rented a charter" and "we had a private floating venue for the evening." For executive-tier events where the privacy, intimacy, and 1:1 conversation quality matter more than venue scale, this is the right boat.

Q2: Can we use the boat for board offsites or leadership retreats?
A2: Yes — frequent booking pattern. Half-day morning or afternoon sessions work well: 3-4 hour blocks with the indoor lounge as the meeting space and the deck for breaks. Bluetooth audio supports presentation playback; Wi-Fi via personal hotspots (the bay has reliable cell signal). Most boards book 4-6 hour blocks to fit a working agenda plus a meal. We can coordinate a working-lunch caterer or a sunset-dinner caterer depending on the session shape.

Q3: What about client entertainment or closing dinners?
A3: The most common executive booking. Catered dinner aboard, BYO premium wine and spirits, sunset on San Diego Bay as the deal-closing backdrop. We coordinate with caterers familiar with yacht delivery (short list available on request). For deal teams of 6-12, the venue is the differentiator — the client remembers the night, not the steakhouse. For pure client-side meetings (no deal-team contingent), 4-6 attendees is the typical scale.

Q4: Can we invoice or use a corporate purchase order?
A4: Yes. We accept purchase orders, advance invoicing, and corporate billing. Email info@malarkycharters.com for invoicing setup — we typically process within 24 business hours. Standard payment terms are net 30 for established corporate accounts; deposit-plus-balance for new ones. We can also accommodate split billing if multiple departments or entities are funding the charter.

Q5: How is this different from Triton Charters' corporate offering?
A5: Triton is the right boat for 13-100 guest corporate events — full reception scale, two bars, dance floor, larger crew. Malarky is the right boat for 6-12 guest executive-tier events where the privacy, intimacy, and 1:1 conversation quality matter more than venue scale. Same fleet of operators, different scale category. For your 60-person sales kickoff celebration, book Triton. For your CEO plus 10 direct reports offsite, book Malarky. The deciding question is the guest count.

Q6: Is catering really fully welcome? What's the setup?
A6: Yes. We don't operate a galley service — catering is your responsibility but we make it operationally easy. Partner caterers know the dock and the boat (we'll share a short list at booking). Caterers deliver to Shelter Island marina 30 minutes before departure; we transfer everything aboard. Indoor lounge has bar-height counters that work for buffet setup; cockpit table seats 8 for plated service. We provide ice, cooler, plates, and glassware on request. BYO wine/cocktails coordinate with the catering delivery.

Q7: What's the cancellation policy for executive bookings?
A7: For executive charters specifically: full deposit refund 21+ days prior, free reschedule up to 7 days prior subject to availability, non-refundable within 7 days. Weather-related flexibility is the standard exception on shorter notice. For board offsites or contracted retreats where multiple executives are blocking calendars, we recommend booking 4-6 weeks ahead and adding a soft-hold the same day the calendar invite goes out — we'll hold the date for 48 hours pending confirmation.
```

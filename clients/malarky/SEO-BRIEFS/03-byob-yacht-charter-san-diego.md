## Why this page matters

## FAQ Block — Verbatim Copy

```
Q1: Can I really bring my own alcohol on a yacht charter in San Diego?
A1: Yes — Malarky is one of the only yacht charters in San Diego that allows BYOB. You can bring beer, wine, champagne, spirits, mixers, and non-alcoholic drinks. Two rules: no glass containers (we transfer everything to plastic before boarding) and no red wine (it permanently stains the deck and cushions).

Q2: Why doesn't every yacht charter allow BYOB?
A2: Most San Diego yacht charters hold liquor licenses and operate on a bar-tab model — the bar tab is a major part of how they make money. Malarky doesn't hold a liquor license, which is what allows us to let guests bring their own. It's a structural difference in how the business is set up, not just a policy choice.

Q3: How much money does BYOB actually save?
A3: Realistic savings on a typical 3-hour charter for 12 guests: $400 to $800. A bottle of champagne at Costco runs about $20; the same bottle on a bar-tab charter runs $80 or more. A case of beer is around $25 at retail vs roughly $200 of draft tabs across the same group. The savings compound by guest and by hour.

Q4: Why no red wine?
A4: Red wine permanently stains the white deck and cushions on Malarky — even with immediate cleaning, the pigment soaks in. White wine, rosé, champagne, and sparkling are all welcome. If you must have red, we recommend bringing it to a post-charter dinner instead.

Q5: Why no glass containers?
A5: Two reasons. Safety: glass that breaks on a boat deck is hard to fully clean up and creates injury risk for guests in bare feet. Environmental: a dropped glass bottle goes to the bottom of San Diego Bay and stays there. We transfer any liquid in glass to plastic cups or pitchers before boarding — takes about two minutes per group.

Q6: Do I need to bring my own cooler and ice?
A6: We provide one cooler with ice. If you're bringing a lot of drinks for a larger group, bring an extra cooler — we'll store it on the aft deck. You don't need to bring your own ice; we have it.

Q7: Can I bring food too?
A7: Yes. BYOF is allowed and encouraged. Most groups bring takeout, sandwiches, charcuterie boards, or grocery-store assemblies. We don't cook onboard but our crew will plate, serve, and clean up. If you'd rather have catering arranged, we partner with local San Diego caterers — mention it when you book.

Q8: Can guests get too drunk and ruin the charter?
A8: Captain has full authority for safety. We're permissive with quantity; not permissive with anyone who can't board safely. In rare cases the captain will refuse a clearly intoxicated guest at the dock, before departure. Once underway, the captain's call on the boat is final. The vast majority of charters never see this come up.
```

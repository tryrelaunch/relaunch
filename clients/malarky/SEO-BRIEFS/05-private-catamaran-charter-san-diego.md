## Why "private catamaran charter" matters as a slug rename

**Three reasons:**

1. **Anti-cannibalization with the catamaran head term.** "Catamaran charter san diego" (80/mo, KD 29) is contested across the fleet — Triton, Athena, and Alana all run catamarans. The "private" modifier narrows to Malarky's territory without abandoning the underlying intent. Triton/Athena/Alana can target the generic head term; Malarky owns the "private" version.

2. **Brand-positioning consistency.** Every other Malarky priority page uses "private" framing (private yacht charter, private sunset yacht, etc.). The catamaran page is the outlier — renaming aligns it with the brand voice.

3. **Sailing-catamaran USP exposure.** This page is also where Malarky should hammer the "only sailing catamaran in the fleet" angle. Triton, Athena, Alana, Aquata are all power vessels. Malarky is the only one that actually sails. The slug rename is paired with a content pivot that leads with sailing.

## FAQ Block — Verbatim Copy

```
Q1: What's the difference between a catamaran charter and a regular boat charter?
A1: A catamaran has two hulls instead of one. That changes nearly everything about the experience. Catamarans are wider for the same length, which means more deck space, larger cabins, and three distinct sitting areas instead of one cockpit. They don't heel (tip sideways) the way monohulls do — guests who get seasick on traditional sailboats often have no issues. And on Malarky specifically, you get a 47-foot sailing catamaran, not a motor yacht — meaning quieter, more authentic, and a genuine sailing experience when conditions allow.

Q2: Is Malarky a sailing catamaran or a power catamaran?
A2: Sailing. Malarky is a Voyage 47 performance sailing catamaran, which makes it the only sailing catamaran in our family of San Diego boats. Triton, Athena, Alana, and Aquata are all power vessels. When the wind permits, the captain sails Malarky — engines off, sails up, the sound of wind and water replacing motor noise. When conditions don't favor sailing, the captain motors. You don't have to choose; the captain reads the day and uses whatever fits.

Q3: How big is Malarky and how many guests can charter it?
A3: Malarky is 47 feet long with 4 staterooms, 2 bathrooms, a full indoor lounge, an aft deck with shaded seating for around 8, and a bow trampoline. U.S. Coast Guard certified for up to 12 guests, plus our captain and crew. The vessel is substantial — a real yacht by every common definition — and intentionally limited to 12 guests for the private-charter experience.

Q4: Where does the catamaran go?
A4: We operate exclusively on San Diego Bay. Routes are flexible — most charters take in the Coronado Bridge, USS Midway, Star of India, Seaport Village, and the Embarcadero, with options to anchor at swim spots or photo spots. We do not run open-ocean charters, offshore charters, or Mission Bay charters.

Q5: Wait, isn't the Voyage 47 designed for offshore passages?
A5: Yes — the Voyage 47 is built in Cape Town for blue-water voyaging. The build quality, hardware, and safety systems exceed what most charter boats this size carry. We sail it exclusively on San Diego Bay, where that bluewater-grade build translates into exceptional comfort, stability, and a yacht that operates well above the standard for sheltered-water charters. The pedigree is a feature; the operating envelope is San Diego Bay.

Q6: Is the catamaran private, or do I share it with other groups?
A6: Private only. When you book Malarky, the entire 47-foot catamaran is yours and your group's for the duration of the charter. No shared deck, no other groups boarding, no ticketed strangers. The cap is 12 guests and we don't exceed it.

Q7: Does BYOB really work on a sailing catamaran?
A7: Yes — Malarky is one of the only yacht charters in San Diego that allows BYOB. Bring beer, wine, champagne, spirits, mixers, non-alcoholic, takeout, charcuterie. Two rules: no glass (we transfer to plastic before boarding) and no red wine (it permanently stains the deck). See our full BYOB charter details for specifics.
```

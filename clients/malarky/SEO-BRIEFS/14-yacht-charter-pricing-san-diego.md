## Why Path B specifically

Operator decision (confirmed): pricing varies enough by date/season/day-of-week/add-ons that committing to specific dollars on the page risks (a) misleading customers when actuals differ, (b) violating fleet-rule schema integrity, and (c) creating a quote-then-disappointment dynamic. The Path B page sells *transparency on the structure* (what determines the price, what's included, what's add-on) and converts users to the quote process.

The page's SEO bet is that "yacht charter pricing san diego" search intent is split roughly 50/50 between "I want to see a number" and "I want to understand what's included before I call." The Path B page wins the second half decisively (clear what's-included story, BYOB savings comparison, no per-person minimums, clear quote process) while the call-driven quote process captures the first half via response-time commitments.

## FAQ Block — Verbatim Copy (NO dollar amounts)

```
Q1: Why isn't there a specific price on this page?
A1: Because charter rates honestly vary by duration, day of week, season, and the add-ons you want — and committing to a single number on a page either means we have to overstate it (so the listed number is high enough to cover every scenario) or understate it (so customers find out the real number is higher when they actually book). We'd rather quote your actual all-in number based on your actual booking, and we'll do that within an hour of an inquiry.

Q2: What's actually included in the charter rate?
A2: Every charter includes the full private use of the 47-foot catamaran, the USCG-licensed captain, the crew, fuel, ice, a cooler, glassware on request, 2 bathrooms, the indoor lounge, the sound system, and the captain's positioning of the boat for sunset photos or specific anchor spots you want. There's no mandatory catering package, no per-person drink minimum, no bar tab built into the rate.

Q3: What costs extra (so I'm not surprised at quote time)?
A3: Three categories. (a) Add-ons — water toys (waterslide, lily pad, kayaks, tower pads, paddleboards), photographer-aboard coordination, custom catering coordination. (b) Your spend — food and beverages (BYO, not built into the rate), gratuity (15-20% standard, not built in), any decor you want to bring. (c) Optional services — extended charter duration past your booked block, fuel surcharge in edge-case long-distance routes (rare). We'll itemize all of it in the quote.

Q4: How is BYOB cheaper than a charter with a bar tab?
A4: Charter bars typically price drinks at 3-4x the retail bottle cost. On a premium-bottle charter — champagne, wine, cocktails for 8-12 guests — that markup adds up to $300-500 of difference per evening. BYOB means you buy the bottles at retail (Costco, BevMo, Total Wine) and bring them aboard. Our charter rate is just the charter; your bar spend is yours. For the full breakdown with specific examples, see the BYOB charter page.

Q5: How fast will I get a quote?
A5: Email inquiries get a quote within 1 business hour during operating hours (8am-8pm Pacific). Phone inquiries get a quote in real time when we answer. The FareHarbor booking widget auto-quotes for standard 2-4 hour blocks on available dates — for non-standard durations, custom add-ons, or specific date holds, the email/phone route is faster.

Q6: Do I need to pay a deposit? What's the cancellation policy?
A6: Yes, booking requires a deposit (amount specified in your quote). Full deposit refund if you cancel 3+ weeks before your charter. Free reschedule up to 1 week before, subject to availability. Non-refundable within 1 week. We add weather flexibility on shorter notice — if conditions genuinely impair the day, we move you to the next reasonable window at no charge.
```

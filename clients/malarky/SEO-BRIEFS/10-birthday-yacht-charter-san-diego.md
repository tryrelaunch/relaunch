## FAQ Block — Verbatim Copy

```
Q1: How many guests can come to the birthday charter?
A1: Up to 12 guests total including the birthday person. That's the U.S. Coast Guard limit on Malarky and we don't exceed it. For larger birthdays (13–35 guests with themed packages, or 36–100 guests with a dance floor), our sister vessels Alana Yacht Rental and Triton Charters handle those scales.

Q2: Can we bring a cake and cut it on the boat?
A2: Yes. We have a small fridge to store the cake before serving. Bakery deliveries can drop at the dock before departure (give us a heads up at booking). Candles on the cake are fine when wind is calm; the captain may suggest moving the cake-cutting moment to the indoor lounge or aft deck depending on conditions. Most groups cut the cake on the aft deck — there's a real table there.

Q3: How much does BYOB actually save for a birthday charter?
A3: Realistic savings for a typical 10-guest, 3-hour birthday: $230 to $430. A bottle of champagne at Costco runs about $20 versus $80+ on a bar-tab charter. A bottle of premium spirits is $25–35 retail vs $130+ marked up onboard. The savings often fund a better cake, better catering, or a longer charter. See our BYOB charter page for the full breakdown.

Q4: What's the typical age range for Malarky birthday charters?
A4: Malarky is positioned for adult birthdays — milestone celebrations (30, 40, 50, 60), 21st birthdays, and casual adult birthdays where the inner circle wants something better than a restaurant. The boat features (waterslide, water toys, BYOB) are framed for adult party energy. We're not a kid-party venue; if you're planning a kids' birthday, there are better-suited options elsewhere in San Diego.

Q5: How does the waterslide work at a birthday charter?
A5: The 11-foot inflatable slide deploys off the aft deck into the water at anchor. Captain or crew supervises. It's the social-media reel moment of most adult birthday charters — the "everyone goes down once" group video. Many bachelorettes and milestone birthdays specifically add the slide for the photo content.

Q6: What about decorations — what works on a boat?
A6: Bring whatever fits the theme. Best on a boat: banners (tape to walls, not varnished surfaces), balloons (tie them down — wind), custom cups (NO GLASS), table runners, sashes for the birthday person. What doesn't work: smoke machines, large structural props, anything with adhesive on varnished wood, glass containers (we'd transfer them to plastic before boarding anyway). The bay does most of the visual work — light decor is plenty.

Q7: How long should the birthday charter be?
A7: 3 hours is the sweet spot. Time to depart, cruise out to a swim or anchor spot, deploy water toys or settle in for sunset, have your meal and cake, take photos, and head back unrushed. 4 hours works for longer reception-style meals or groups wanting extended swim time. 2-hour minimum is fine for very small intimate birthdays (4–6 guests) but feels short for full party energy.
```

## Why this page needs the reposition

The current page is well-written but generic. Three problems:

1. **Doesn't differentiate from Alana's themed-bachelorette lane.** Alana is positioned as the disco-themed / pink-themed / blue-themed party-boat bachelorette (35 guests, bartender, full party setup). The current Malarky page reads like a smaller version of the same thing. The two should target different bachelorette buyers entirely — Alana = party-boat-themed, Malarky = intimate-premium-BYOB.

2. **Buries the BYOB cost-savings story.** Bachelorettes are budget-sensitive (typically the bride doesn't pay; the maid of honor coordinates and the group splits). BYOB savings on champagne alone are $300–500 per bachelorette vs the bar-tab competition. This should be a major section, not a one-line mention.

3. **Misses the photography angle.** Bachelorettes generate more social-media content than any other charter type. The page should explicitly explain the photo logistics (which spaces work for which shots, which times of day, golden hour positioning) — that's part of why this product wins vs alternatives.

## FAQ Block — Verbatim Copy

```
Q1: What makes Malarky different from other San Diego bachelorette boat options?
A1: Two things. First, the boat is fully private — your group has all 47 feet of catamaran to yourselves, with no other guests onboard. Second, we allow BYOB, which the bar-tab charter models don't. That combination — private and BYOB — is what most bachelorettes are actually looking for: the social-media-quality photo backdrop, premium drinks at retail prices, and no other groups in the way. If your group is larger than 12 or wants a themed party with a bartender, our sister vessel Alana Yacht Rental handles that lane (up to 35 guests, themed packages, full bar).

Q2: How many guests can come on the bachelorette?
A2: Up to 12 guests including the bride. That's the U.S. Coast Guard limit on Malarky and we don't exceed it. For groups of 13–35, see Alana Yacht Rental (themed bachelorette packages). For 36–100, Triton Charters (75-foot catamaran with bartender and dance floor). Same San Diego Bay, different boats for different bachelorette scales.

Q3: How much does BYOB actually save?
A3: For a typical 10-guest, 3-hour bachelorette: $300 to $500 versus the bar-tab model. Costco champagne runs about $20 a bottle versus $80+ on a charter bar tab. A case of beer at retail is $25 vs roughly $200 of bar drafts spread across the group. Premium spirits — Tito's, Grey Goose, whatever — are $25-35 a bottle at retail vs $130+ marked up onboard. The savings fund a better dinner, longer charter, or just stay in pocket.

Q4: What about food? Can we bring snacks and meals?
A4: Yes — BYOF is fully welcome. Most bachelorettes do a grocery-store charcuterie spread, sandwiches or wraps, plus a custom cake or cupcakes for the bride. Restaurant takeout works great (Filippi's, Hodad's, Phil's BBQ all travel well). If you'd rather have it catered, we partner with local San Diego caterers — mention catering when you book and we'll send menu and pricing options.

Q5: What time of day should we book?
A5: Depends on the vibe. **Daytime bachelorettes** (noon-3pm) lean swim, water toys, sun-drinking energy. **Late afternoon** (3-6pm bleeding into sunset) gives you both daytime activity AND golden-hour photos. **Sunset-only** (5-8pm) is the elegant champagne-and-skyline version. Most bachelorettes book 4-hour charters that combine swim time with sunset photos. Saturday afternoon slots in summer book 6-8 weeks ahead.

Q6: Can we use the water toys?
A6: Yes — paddleboards, lily pad, kayaks, and the 11-foot waterslide are all available as add-ons. The slide is by far the most-photographed moment of bachelorette charters (the "everyone goes down the slide one by one" reel). Mention which toys you want when you book; we'll have them ready. See the water toys page for full details.

Q7: What about decorations — can we bring sashes, banners, balloons?
A7: Yes — bring whatever you want. The boat is already a great backdrop, so most groups go light on decor — matching cups (NO GLASS), sashes for the bride and bridesmaids, maybe a banner taped to the aft deck wall, and that's plenty. Save the heavy decor energy for the photo content, not the boat dressing. We do ask: nothing that needs adhesive on varnished surfaces, no smoke machines, no large structural props.

Q8: What's the cancellation policy and how far ahead should we book?
A8: Saturday and Sunday afternoon slots in summer book 6 to 8 weeks ahead — reserve early if your date is set. Spring/fall typically 2-4 weeks ahead. Winter often flexible 1-2 weeks. Cancellation: full deposit refund if you cancel 3+ weeks before your charter, free reschedule up to 1 week before departure, non-refundable within 1 week. Reschedules subject to availability.
```

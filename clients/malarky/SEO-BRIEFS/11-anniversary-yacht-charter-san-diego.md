## FAQ Block — Verbatim Copy

```
Q1: What's a typical anniversary charter on Malarky?
A1: Most anniversaries book a 2.5 to 3-hour evening charter timed to catch the sunset. The pattern: depart roughly 2 to 2.5 hours before sunset, position near the Coronado Bridge for golden-hour photos, anchor for a quiet drink or meal, then cruise back through the blue hour. Couples ranges from 2 (just the two of you) to 12 (including extended family for milestone anniversaries). The captain runs the boat; you bring the champagne, the dinner, and the people.

Q2: How is this different from "anniversary date ideas" on land?
A2: A restaurant or a hotel anniversary night is a meal or a stay — bookended by the rest of your day. A yacht charter is the event itself; once you leave the dock, the next 3 hours are entirely contained, just your group on the water with the bay as the venue. It's the difference between a nice dinner and an actual milestone marker. If you're still in the planning phase looking at the broader options, our sister site Alana Yacht Rental has a longer anniversary ideas guide covering on-land options that pair well with a charter.

Q3: How many guests can come?
A3: Up to 12 guests on Malarky. Most anniversary charters book in two configurations: couples-only (just the two of you, full 12-guest cap unused but yours) or 6–12 guests for milestone anniversaries with extended family. For anniversaries with 13–35 guests, our sister vessel Alana Yacht Rental handles that scale. For 36+, Triton Charters.

Q4: Can we bring champagne?
A4: Yes — BYOB is allowed and actively encouraged for anniversaries. Bring whatever champagne or wine the milestone deserves — premium bottles at retail prices rather than marked up 3-4x on a charter bar tab. Two rules: no glass containers (we transfer to plastic before boarding) and no red wine (it permanently stains the deck). Champagne, white wine, rosé, sparkling, and spirits are all welcome.

Q5: Can the charter be a surprise?
A5: Yes — we coordinate surprise anniversary charters often. Let us know when you book that it's a surprise. We'll work with you on embarkation timing (sometimes a guest is led to the dock under a different pretext), on whether the crew greets the partner formally or quietly, and on any other reveal logistics. The captain and crew are good at reading the moment.

Q6: What about photography?
A6: The bay at golden hour is the photographer's win — the Coronado Bridge as the western frame, the downtown skyline behind, the bow trampoline as the couple-portrait location. If you're hiring a photographer, send them a quick brief on the three boat spaces and the timing; they'll do the rest. We can also recommend local pros who've shot anniversary charters on Malarky.

Q7: What's the cancellation policy and how far ahead should we book?
A7: Anniversary dates often lock in early — if your anniversary is on a specific calendar date (vs. a flexible weekend), book 6+ weeks ahead, especially in summer. Cancellation: full deposit refund if you cancel 3+ weeks before your charter, free reschedule up to 1 week before departure, non-refundable within 1 week. Reschedules subject to availability.
```

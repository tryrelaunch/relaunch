## FAQ Block — Verbatim Copy

```
Q1: What's a micro wedding on a yacht?
A1: A wedding with a small, curated guest list — typically 2 to 20 people — held aboard a private yacht instead of at a venue. On Malarky, the cap is 12 guests including the couple, which fits the elopement-to-micro-wedding range perfectly. The ceremony happens on the bow at golden hour, the reception on the aft deck, the dance moment in the indoor lounge. No venue rental, no setup-strike, no "minimum guest count" pressure — just the people who actually matter on a 47-foot catamaran on San Diego Bay.

Q2: How many guests can attend a Malarky wedding?
A2: Up to 12 guests including the couple. That's the U.S. Coast Guard cap on the vessel, and we don't exceed it — by design. If your wedding is larger than 12 guests, our sister vessel Triton Charters can host up to 100 on a 75-foot catamaran with bartender and dance floor — same San Diego Bay, different scale. We'd rather send you to the right boat than try to make Malarky work for a 50-guest wedding.

Q3: Can the captain officiate?
A3: Our captain is not a wedding officiant. You'll need to bring your own officiant (or have a friend get ordained online — California's friendly to one-day officiants). We can recommend local San Diego officiants who've worked Malarky weddings before if you don't have someone in mind. Ask at booking.

Q4: What's included in a wedding charter?
A4: The entire 47-foot Voyage catamaran exclusively for your wedding party — bow trampoline (ceremony), aft deck (reception), indoor lounge (dance / refresh). USCG-licensed captain and crew for the duration. Sound system (bring a playlist on your phone). Lifejackets, ice, cooler. BYOB and BYOF allowed (no glass, no red wine). Catering can be arranged through our partner local caterers if you'd rather not BYO — mention it when you book.

Q5: How long is a typical wedding charter?
A5: Three hours is the sweet spot for a micro wedding: 60 min cruise + cocktail hour → ceremony at golden hour (~15 min) → reception meal → blue-hour return. Four hours works if you want a longer reception. Elopements with very small parties (2–6 guests) sometimes book the 2-hour minimum, which gives ceremony + champagne + photos and time to head back.

Q6: What about photography?
A6: The bay at golden hour is the photographer's win. Bow trampoline for ceremony shots, Coronado Bridge as the western frame, downtown skyline behind. We can recommend local photographers who've shot Malarky weddings — they know the light and the angles. If you're bringing your own, send them a quick brief on the three spaces and the timing; they'll do the rest.

Q7: Can we customize the route or location?
A7: Within reason. The captain proposes a route based on the day's conditions and your priorities — typical pattern is depart the Shelter Island dock, position near the Coronado Bridge for the ceremony, anchor for the reception, return at blue hour. We can adjust for specific photo spots or timing. We operate exclusively on San Diego Bay — no Mission Bay, no open ocean.

Q8: How far in advance should I book?
A8: Weekend evening slots in summer book 4 to 8 weeks ahead. Spring and fall are typically 2 to 4 weeks ahead. Winter weddings (still beautiful — the bay clears up in winter) are usually flexible 1 to 2 weeks ahead. If your date is set, book as early as possible. Cancellation policy: full deposit refund 3+ weeks before charter; free reschedule up to 1 week prior; non-refundable within 1 week.
```

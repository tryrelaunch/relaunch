═══ CATEGORY 1: BOOKING, QUOTES & CANCELLATION ═══

Q1: How do I get a quote?
A1: Three channels. The FareHarbor booking widget auto-quotes for standard 2-4 hour blocks on available dates. Email info@malarkycharters.com with your date, duration, guest count, and any add-ons — we respond within 1 business hour during operating hours (8am-8pm Pacific). Or call (844) 724-5787 for real-time pricing. Phone is best for complex inquiries with multiple add-ons or specific date holds.

Q2: How far ahead should I book?
A2: It depends on date specificity. If your date is locked (wedding day, specific anniversary date, milestone birthday on the actual date), book 6+ weeks ahead, especially May-October. If your date is flexible (couples-only anniversary on a flexible weekend, executive offsite with calendar control), 2-3 weeks ahead is usually fine for midweek slots, 4-6 weeks for weekend slots. Last-minute slots (1-2 weeks out) often have availability midweek and in shoulder season.

Q3: What's the deposit and how much?
A3: Booking requires a deposit; the amount is specified in your quote and varies by charter size and date proximity. Deposit is fully refundable if you cancel 3+ weeks before your charter date.

Q4: What's the cancellation policy?
A4: Full deposit refund if you cancel 3+ weeks before your charter. Free reschedule up to 1 week before, subject to availability on the new date. Non-refundable within 1 week of the charter, except for weather-related cancellations. We add weather flexibility on shorter notice — if conditions genuinely impair the day, we move you to the next reasonable window at no charge.

Q5: Can I reschedule if my plans change?
A5: Yes — free reschedule up to 1 week before departure, subject to availability on the new date. Within 1 week of the charter, rescheduling is at our discretion and may incur fees depending on how much advance notice we have to fill or hold the original slot.

═══ CATEGORY 2: THE BOAT — SPECS, CAPACITY & ACCESSIBILITY ═══

Q6: What kind of boat is Malarky exactly?
A6: A 47-foot Voyage performance sailing catamaran, built in Cape Town, South Africa. Two hulls, four staterooms below deck, two heads (bathrooms), an indoor air-conditioned salon with bar-height counters, an aft deck cockpit with seating for 8 at the table, and a forward bow trampoline. USCG-certified for up to 12 guests on private charters.

Q7: How many guests can Malarky hold?
A7: Twelve maximum (USCG cap). Most charters book in two scales: couples-only or 2-4 guests for the privacy-oriented bookings, or 6-12 guests for milestone events, executive groups, weddings, bachelorettes, and similar. Twelve is a hard cap; for larger groups, our sister vessel Alana Yacht Rental handles 13-35, and Triton Charters handles 36-100.

Q8: Is the boat appropriate for kids?
A8: Malarky's positioning is adult charters — milestone birthdays, anniversaries, executive events, weddings, bachelorettes, proposals. Kids are not forbidden, but the boat isn't built or staffed for kid-party use cases. Family charters where one or two minors are part of an otherwise-adult group are fine; charters built around a kids' birthday or kid-day-out are a poor fit. For family-with-kids day cruises specifically, our sister vessel Athena Boat Rental is a better fit.

Q9: Is the boat accessible for guests with mobility considerations?
A9: The aft deck cockpit and the indoor lounge are accessible from the dock with a step-up (we can deploy a small portable step or ramp on request). Going below to the staterooms or heads requires stairs that don't accommodate wheelchairs or limited-mobility guests. The aft deck head (in the cockpit) is the primary bathroom for most charters and is accessible from the main deck. Mention any mobility considerations at booking and we'll talk through specifics.

═══ CATEGORY 3: BYOB & CATERING ═══

Q10: What can we bring to drink?
A10: Wide-open BYOB. Champagne, white wine, ros&eacute;, sparkling wine, beer, spirits, cocktails, non-alcoholic — all welcome. Two rules: no glass containers at boarding (we transfer wine and champagne to plastic at the dock for safety), and no red wine on deck (it permanently stains the gelcoat; we don't make exceptions). We provide ice, a cooler, and glassware on request.

Q11: Can we bring food?
A11: Yes — fully welcome. Bring whatever you'd want to eat. Charcuterie, sandwiches, a catered spread, full multi-course meals from a partner caterer. We provide trays, plates, and utensils on request. The cockpit table seats 8; the indoor lounge has bar-height counters that work for buffet setup.

Q12: Can we have catering brought directly to the boat?
A12: Yes. We have a short list of partner caterers familiar with the Shelter Island dock and the boat — mention catering interest at booking and we'll share contacts. Caterers deliver to the marina 30 minutes before departure; we transfer everything aboard. We don't charge a coordination fee; the catering itself is contracted directly between you and the caterer.

Q13: Can we bring a birthday cake or wedding cake?
A13: Absolutely. Bring whatever cake the occasion calls for; we provide a tray and storage in the mini fridge until it's time. The aft deck cockpit table is the typical cake-cutting setup; for larger group photos, the indoor lounge counter works well.

Q14: What's not allowed?
A14: Red wine on deck (permanent staining). Glass containers at boarding (we transfer to plastic at the dock). Illegal substances. Black-soled shoes (scuff the deck — soft-soled shoes or barefoot is fine). Smoking inside the cabin (open-deck smoking is allowed). Drone launches without permits.

═══ CATEGORY 4: CHARTER TYPES & SCENARIOS ═══

Q15: Which Malarky charter is right for my event?
A15: Couples anniversaries, proposals, intimate weddings, and milestone birthdays each have a dedicated page with the specific framing for that use case. Executive entertaining and board offsites have an executive page. Bachelorette parties (intimate scale) have their own page. The water-toys-day use case has its own page. Pricing and deals each have their own page. The relevant page for your event is likely linked in the nav menu under "Charter Types."

Q16: Can the charter be a surprise?
A16: Yes — we coordinate surprise charters often. Surprise proposals (the most common), surprise anniversaries, surprise milestone birthdays. Let us know at booking that it's a surprise; we'll work with you on embarkation timing, crew greeting style (formal welcome vs. quiet boarding), and any reveal logistics. The crew is good at reading the moment.

Q17: My group is larger than 12 — what should I do?
A17: For groups of 13-35, our sister vessel Alana Yacht Rental is the right boat — 35-guest power catamaran with a bartender. For groups of 36-100, Triton Charters is the right boat — 75-foot catamaran with two bartenders and a dance floor. Same fleet of operators. We can route your inquiry to the right boat if you call us; we won't try to fit you onto Malarky if the scale isn't right.

Q18: My event is on a specific date — what if you're booked?
A18: We hold dates strictly to one booking at a time. If your specific date is booked, we'll suggest the nearest available dates that fit your duration and time-of-day preferences. For date-locked events (weddings, milestone anniversaries on the actual day), we recommend booking 6+ weeks ahead in peak season. If your preferred date is gone, we may also have availability on a sister vessel (Alana, Triton) if your scale matches.

═══ CATEGORY 5: CAPTAIN, CREW & DISCRETION ═══

Q19: Who's the captain?
A19: USCG-licensed, with hundreds of charter days on Malarky specifically. The captain handles boat operation, routing, anchor positioning for sunset photos, weather decisions, and any operational coordination throughout your charter. For executive bookings, the captain signs NDAs on request.

Q20: What does the crew do?
A20: The crew (typically one crew member in addition to the captain) helps with pouring, plating, serving, and general boat operations during the charter. Not a waiter — more of a discreet operations hand who's there when you need help and out of frame when you don't. The crew is discretion-trained for proposal, executive, and similar private bookings: they don't acknowledge what they overhear, don't comment on the guest list, and don't post booking content to social media.

Q21: How much should I tip the captain and crew?
A21: Captain-and-crew gratuity is appreciated but not built into the charter rate. Standard range on charters is 15-20% of the charter rate, similar to a restaurant tip. Some guests handle this in cash at the end of the charter; some add it to the booking total via the booking system. Both are fine. Discretionary, not required.

═══ CATEGORY 6: WATER TOYS & ADD-ONS ═══

Q22: What water toys do you offer?
A22: Five categories: an 11-foot waterslide that deploys off the side of the boat, a 15-foot lily pad (floating platform that anchors alongside), single and tandem kayaks, stand-up paddleboards, and tower pads for diving. All available as add-ons priced separately and quoted at booking. Bundling multiple toys for a single charter is meaningfully cheaper per-toy than adding them individually.

Q23: Are there age requirements for the water toys?
A23: The waterslide and tower pads have minimum height/swimming-skill requirements typical of any pool slide — kids who can swim independently are fine; very young kids should stay on the lily pad or paddleboards under supervision. We provide life vests in adult and child sizes. Mention guest ages at booking and we'll guide on which toys work best for the group.

Q24: When do water toys not work?
A24: Wind chop above moderate (small craft advisory level) makes the slide and tower pads unsafe to deploy — we'll typically swap to lily-pad-only or skip the in-water portion entirely. Mid-day is the best window for water toys (water warmest, light best, group in actual swim mode). Sunset and after-dark charters typically skip water toys; the slide doesn't work after dark for safety reasons.

═══ CATEGORY 7: WEATHER, PARKING & LOGISTICS ═══

Q25: What happens if it rains?
A25: Light drizzle or overcast usually proceeds — San Diego rain is typically brief and the indoor lounge is fully air-conditioned with the rain-friendly option. Heavy sustained rain, small craft advisories, dangerous wind, or fog impairing the bridge backdrop on a sunset charter — the captain calls it and we move you to the next reasonable window at no charge. "Genuinely impair" is the threshold; we don't cancel for a few clouds.

Q26: Where do we park?
A26: Free guest parking at the Shelter Island marina, 2700 Shelter Island Dr, San Diego 92106. Adjacent to the dock. Black-car drop-off and pickup are straightforward; the dock is clearly marked. We send detailed dock instructions in your booking confirmation.

Q27: Where exactly is the dock?
A27: The dock is on the back side of the marina behind Ketch Kitchen and Taps restaurant. Walk through the marina parking lot toward the water; you'll see the slip clearly marked. If you arrive 15 minutes before your charter, the crew will be on the dock and visible from the parking lot.

Q28: What time should we arrive?
A28: Fifteen minutes before your scheduled charter departure time. Earlier than that and the boat may not be ready for boarding; later than that compresses your charter time. The captain departs on schedule because charter windows are timed to specific light (golden hour, anchor windows). If you're running late, call (844) 724-5787 from the road so we can adjust expectations.

Q29: What should we wear?
A29: Layers. San Diego bay in the afternoon is warm; the same bay after sunset is meaningfully cooler with the breeze. A light jacket or sweater is never a bad call for evening charters. Soft-soled shoes (boat shoes, sneakers with light soles) or barefoot — no black-soled shoes that mark the deck. Sunscreen is essential even on overcast days; the water reflects UV. Sunglasses.

Q30: What about photographers, drones, or filming?
A30: Photographers aboard are welcome (mention at booking — they board as one of your guests). Drone photography in San Diego Bay requires proper permits; if you want a drone aerial of the charter, ask in advance and we'll advise on permit logistics. Cell signal across the bay is reliable, so video calls and live streams work without issue if that's part of your charter (executive presentations, virtual surprise reveals, etc.).

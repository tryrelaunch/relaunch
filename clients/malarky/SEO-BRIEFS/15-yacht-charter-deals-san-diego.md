## FAQ Block — Verbatim Copy (NO dollar amounts)

```
Q1: Do you run sales or promotional discounts?
A1: Not in the flash-sale or Black-Friday-style format. Charter rates already vary structurally by date, day of week, and season, so a percent-off promotion on top of that variable rate would mostly create confusion. What we do is quote our actual all-in rate at booking time, factoring in midweek / shoulder-season / mid-day adjustments where they apply. If you want the lowest possible rate on a flexible date, a Tuesday afternoon in February is the structural answer.

Q2: How much can I save by booking midweek instead of weekend?
A2: Meaningful, but the exact delta depends on the date and season. Monday through Thursday rates are lower than Friday-through-Sunday rates across every season; the gap is widest on Saturday-evening peak slots in summer (where the weekend premium stacks with the peak-hour premium) and narrowest on Sunday morning shoulder-season slots. For most couples-only charters with flexible dates, a Tuesday or Wednesday evening will save enough to fund a meaningful add-on (water toys, photographer) compared to the same charter on Saturday.

Q3: Is the bay actually nice in November-April (shoulder season)?
A3: Yes. San Diego doesn't have a winter in the way most of the country does. November and February afternoons are some of the cleanest sailing days of the year — typically less wind chop, better visibility on the bridge backdrop, and meaningfully softer crowds on the water. Daytime temperatures are usually 60-70F. The indoor air-conditioned lounge handles any chill once the sun drops, and most charter use cases (executive offsites, anniversaries, casual sunset cruises) don't depend on summer warmth.

Q4: Are there package deals for water toys?
A4: Yes — bundling multiple water toys for a single charter is meaningfully better per-toy than adding them one at a time. The full package (waterslide + lily pad + kayaks + paddleboards + tower pads) makes the most structural sense for 4-6 hour mid-day charters where the group will actually use all of them. Mention "full water-toys package" at booking and we'll quote it as a bundle.

Q5: How can I find out about last-minute open slots?
A5: Two places. (a) The FareHarbor booking widget shows real-time availability — if a weekend block opens up because of a reschedule, it appears there immediately. (b) Our Instagram (@malarkycharters) is where we occasionally post last-minute open-slot announcements when a peak block becomes available. We don't run an email newsletter or notification list — we'd rather not add to the inbox volume, and the real-time booking widget makes the same information available without a subscription step.
```

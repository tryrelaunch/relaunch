## Two existing copy errors that need fixing in this rewrite

**Error 1: "Blue-water catamaran" hero copy.** Current hero says *"Malarky is 47 feet of blue-water catamaran..."*. Per fleet rules, "blue water" framing is permitted only as **boat pedigree** ("built in Cape Town as a blue-water vessel" = describing the boat's build quality), never as charter context. The current hero conflates the two. Reword to keep the pedigree story without implying open-ocean charters: *"Malarky is a 47-foot Voyage performance catamaran — Cape Town-built for blue-water voyaging, chartered exclusively on San Diego Bay."* Pedigree preserved, scope clear.

**Error 2: "Crew can serve as a bartender on request."** Per fleet rules (`00-FLEET-RULES.md` Section 1), Malarky has no bartender / no bar service — that's a Triton/Athena/Alana feature. Malarky is BYOB only with crew (not a dedicated bartender). Correct framing: *"Your crew helps pour, plate, and serve throughout the charter — concierge-style support for whatever you've brought aboard."* Acknowledges crew service without claiming a formal bartender role they don't fill.

These two corrections are required. They're tagged in the acceptance checklist below.

## FAQ Block — Verbatim Copy

```
Q1: What makes Malarky a "luxury" yacht rental in San Diego?
A1: A few things. The boat itself is a 47-foot Voyage performance catamaran — Cape Town-built for blue-water voyaging, which translates to a wider beam, more interior volume, and better build quality than the average charter vessel. The interior has 4 actual staterooms, 2 bathrooms, an indoor lounge with modern decor and a mini fridge. The aft deck has shaded seating for around 8. The bow has a trampoline that hangs over the water. And the charter model is exclusive — your group books the entire boat. Twelve guests, captain and crew, no shared cruise.

Q2: How is this different from luxury charters that hold 30 to 100 guests?
A2: Different definition of luxury. A 100-guest catamaran with a bartender, dance floor, and DJ is a luxury party experience — it's the right call for a milestone party or a 60-guest wedding. Malarky is the opposite end of the same spectrum: luxury through intimacy. Twelve guests max, your crew knows everyone's name by hour two, the music is yours, the pace is yours, the conversations don't have to compete with three other groups. Different boats for different occasions; we recommend our sister vessels Triton (up to 100) and Alana (up to 35) when group size doesn't fit Malarky.

Q3: What's included with a Malarky luxury charter?
A3: The whole boat (47 feet, 12-guest exclusive), USCG-approved captain, full crew, indoor lounge with mini fridge, aft deck seating, bow trampoline, paddleboards, lily pad, kayaks, and tower pads as optional add-ons. The 11-foot waterslide is also available as an add-on for groups that want it. BYOB and BYOF are allowed — bring your own beer, wine, champagne, spirits, snacks, takeout, whatever. The crew helps pour, plate, and serve throughout the charter. Catering can be arranged through our partner local caterers — mention it at booking.

Q4: Can guests really bring their own alcohol on a luxury charter?
A4: Yes. Malarky is one of the only yacht charters in San Diego that allows BYOB, and it's a feature, not a compromise. Bring premium spirits, top-shelf champagne, your favorite craft beer — at retail prices instead of marked up 3 to 4x on a bar tab. The two rules: no glass (we transfer to plastic before boarding) and no red wine (it permanently stains the deck). See our full BYOB charter details if you want the specifics.

Q5: How much does a luxury yacht rental cost in San Diego?
A5: Charters are quote-based and depend on duration, day of week, and time of year. The minimum is 2 hours; we recommend 3 to 4 hours for the typical experience. See our pricing page for ranges, or call (844) 724-5787 and we'll send you a quote with options for your date.

Q6: How long is a typical luxury charter?
A6: Two-hour minimum, but most luxury charters book 3 to 4 hours. That gives time to ease out into the bay, anchor at a swim or photo spot, set up any add-ons, eat and drink at pace, watch the city pass by, and head back without feeling rushed. Anything shorter feels like you're racing the clock.

Q7: What's your cancellation policy?
A7: Full deposit refund if you cancel 3 or more weeks before your charter. Free reschedule up to 1 week before departure. Within 1 week, the reservation is non-refundable. Reschedules to later dates are subject to availability.
```

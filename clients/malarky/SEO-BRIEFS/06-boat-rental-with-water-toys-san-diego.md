## FAQ Block — Verbatim Copy

```
Q1: What water toys does Malarky offer?
A1: Five options as add-ons: an 11-foot waterslide that deploys off the aft deck, stand-up paddleboards, a 15-foot lily pad floating platform, sit-on-top kayaks (single and tandem), and tower pads (padded platforms for diving and jumping from height). All are available on private 12-guest charters; mention which ones you want when you book and we'll have them ready.

Q2: Is the waterslide really 11 feet?
A2: Yes. Eleven feet of inflatable slide that mounts at the aft deck and drops you into the water. It's the most-requested add-on and the most photographed moment of most charters. Adults love it; kids 5 and up can use it with a lifejacket. Captain or crew supervises.

Q3: Do I need to know how to use any of this?
A3: No. Paddleboards and kayaks are beginner-friendly — our crew gives a quick 2-minute how-to before you go in the water. The slide is a slide (no learning curve). The lily pad is a floating platform you climb onto. Tower pads have a brief safety walkthrough before use. None of these require prior experience.

Q4: How much do water toys cost?
A4: Each add-on is priced separately. Mention which toys you want when you book and we'll send the pricing with your quote. Bundled options are sometimes available depending on the charter — ask. The 11-foot waterslide is the most popular and typically the headline add-on.

Q5: Where on San Diego Bay do you use the water toys?
A5: At anchor. The captain picks a spot based on the day's conditions — wind, tide, water temperature, traffic. Common anchorages include the Coronado Cays area, Glorietta Bay just north of Coronado, and sheltered zones behind Shelter Island. Each is roughly 30 minutes from the dock. You can request a specific anchorage, but the captain has the final call on what's safe and feasible that day.

Q6: Can kids use the water toys?
A6: Yes, with rules. Kids 5 and up can use the waterslide and lily pad with a lifejacket. The lower tower pad is supervised; the higher one is adult-only. Paddleboards and kayaks require basic swimming ability plus a lifejacket. The captain or crew supervises water-toy time when kids are involved. Families with kids are a big part of who books with us — we know how to make it work.

Q7: What if the weather isn't great for water toys?
A7: The captain decides on the day. If wind, swell, or water temperature aren't safe or enjoyable, the toys stay onboard and we'd recommend planning the charter around scenic cruising and lounging instead. We don't deploy toys when it isn't safe, and we don't try to talk anyone into using them. If conditions only allow some of the toys (e.g., paddleboards fine, slide too rough), we'll do what works.
```

Meet Malarky

The boat, up close.

Malarky is a private 47-foot Voyage catamaran on San Diego Bay. The whole boat is yours — four staterooms, two bathrooms, room for up to twelve guests, reserved exclusively for your group for the duration of your charter.

Every charter runs with a U.S. Coast Guard-licensed captain and a friendly crew who handle the boat, the route, and the hospitality. The captain's fee is separate — $50/hour, paid directly to the captain via Apple Pay, Venmo, PayPal, or cash upon arrival. You don't need any sailing experience and you don't need a license. You and your guests step aboard at our Shelter Island dock and the rest of the day unfolds.

Malarky is one of the only private yacht charters in San Diego that lets you bring your own drinks and food. Beer, champagne, charcuterie, catering — your choice. A couple of simple rules apply (laid out below). Beyond that, we hand the day over to you.

The Boat: Malarky

A 47-foot Voyage catamaran, built for the ocean — sailing your bay.

Malarky is a Voyage 47 — built in Cape Town, South Africa, by a shipyard that builds boats for blue-water crossings. That pedigree is the reason the boat sits the way it does on a flat-water bay: wide on the beam, low to the surface, almost no roll. Drinks stay where you put them. Guests in heels don’t have to brace against the rail.

Inside, four staterooms and two heads. A full indoor lounge with comfortable seating, natural light through bay-side windows, a coffee table, and a stocked mini fridge for keeping your drinks and snacks cold. Step aft and you'll find a covered deck with table seating for around eight — the most-used spot for meals and conversation, fully shaded against the San Diego sun.

Forward, the bow trampoline net stretches between the hulls. It's where guests stretch out, watch the bow wake, and — frequently — spot dolphins pacing the boat.

The Experience

Your San Diego Bay charter, start to finish.

Charters depart from America's Cup Harbor. Park at 2700 Shelter Island Dr, then walk down the walkway between Intrepid Boat Works and San Diego Marine Exchange to the water. Meet us at the gate to our dock — look for the big Malarky sign above it. (Not the office.) From the harbor mouth, the bay opens in three directions, and your route is whatever you and the captain decide it should be.

Our captains will take you to the same set of San Diego icons: the Coronado Bridge stretched against the sky, the USS Midway moored at the Embarcadero, the Star of India sailing-ship museum, Seaport Village, the silhouette of Cabrillo National Monument on the western point, and the marinas of Shelter Island and Harbor Island. The captain will suggest a route based on the time of day and the weather. You can request specific spots, an anchor stop for a swim, a lap past a particular landmark for photos, or simply "take us where the views are best."

San Diego Bay is unexpectedly rich for wildlife. Dolphin pods are common, especially near the bow trampoline. Sea lions lounge on the buoys. In winter and early spring, gray whales and the occasional blue whale pass within sight of the bay's mouth, and seals are year-round residents. The captain will point out anything worth seeing as you go.

Water Toys & Add-Ons

Anchor at a swim spot. The boat becomes a private water park.

San Diego Bay has a handful of protected spots where the captain will drop anchor for a swim break. That's when the toys come out:

  • Stand-up paddleboards for cruising the calm water around the boat
  • Kayaks for paired or solo paddling
  • Lily pad — a 16-foot floating mat that fits everyone aboard
  • Tower pads for kids and adults to jump from

The 15-foot lily pad is one of a kind on San Diego Bay — most charter boats don't have the deck space or the crew to deploy it safely. Add-ons are confirmed in advance, so we'll have everything inflated and ready when you anchor. See the full lineup on the boat rental with water toys page.

Catering Available on Request

Light bites to plated meals, or bring your own.

We work with a roster of San Diego caterers and can arrange anything from a charcuterie spread to a fully plated multi-course meal. Mention catering when you book and we'll send menu and pricing options that match the size of your group, the time of day, and any dietary needs.

Or BYOF — bring your own food. Many guests pick up takeout from a favorite spot in town and keep it simple. Beer, white wine, champagne, cocktails in cans, hot food in non-glass containers — all welcome aboard.

Transparent Pricing

Hourly rates, no hidden fees, quote on request.

Charter pricing is structured around hourly blocks with a two-hour minimum, though three to four hours is what most guests book — enough time to take in the bay, anchor for a swim, eat and drink, and return without rushing.

Pricing is a flat hourly rate — same Saturday sunset or Tuesday morning, summer or winter. Add-ons are itemized so you only pay for what you use. What changes by season isn't price — it's availability. Summer (May through October) books out months ahead, so reserve early if you want first pick of sunset Saturdays.

What a captained charter means

A captained charter means a professional mariner handles everything nautical so your group can focus entirely on the experience. Your captain decides the safest and most scenic route for the day’s conditions; your crew handles lines, safety equipment, and anything else that needs hands-on attention.

You are not required to participate in sailing operations. You don’t need sailing experience, a boating license, or any maritime knowledge. The captain is a professional — USCG licensed, familiar with San Diego Bay’s tides, winds, and traffic — and genuinely enjoyable company. Captains on Malarky know the bay well enough to point out things you wouldn’t notice on your own.

The route is flexible

Unlike some charters that run the same loop regardless, Malarky’s captain will recommend a route based on the day’s wind and tide, then adjust based on what your group wants. Want to see the Coronado Bridge up close? Want to anchor near Silver Strand and swim? Want to ghost along the North Bay into Mission Bay? Ask. The captain will tell you what’s realistic and make it happen if it is.

Captain and crew — what’s the difference?

The captain holds the USCG license and commands the vessel — navigation, safety decisions, and communication with the harbor. The crew assists with lines and guest experience. On most Malarky charters, a crew member also serves as a bartender on request. This is standard for private charters at this level.

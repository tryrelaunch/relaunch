The Bay

Cruise San Diego Bay.

San Diego Bay is one of the calmest and most scenic boating destinations in California, which is why we run every Malarky charter here. During your cruise, you'll usually pass:

  • Coronado Bridge, one of San Diego's most iconic landmarks and the best spot on the bay for sunset photos.
  • USS Midway, the massive historic aircraft carrier docked along the downtown waterfront, an incredible backdrop as you cruise past.
  • Downtown skyline, the city lights start reflecting across the water as golden hour comes in.
  • Seaport Village, the lively waterfront area filled with restaurants, shops, and classic harbor scenes.
  • Shelter Island & Harbor Island, marina-lined sections of the bay known for luxury yachts and waterfront views.
  • Star of India, the world's oldest active sailing ship, docked at the Embarcadero.

Dolphin sightings are common during charters, especially up at the bow trampoline where guests often watch them swim through the wake just feet away. Your captain will adjust the route based on the day's conditions and what your group's enjoying.

On the Water

Anchor, swim, and relax.

Most longer charters include time to anchor in calm water so your group can swim, float, and enjoy the bay at your own pace. Add-ons like paddleboards and the floating lily pad turn your charter into a full San Diego boat day.

What guests usually do during anchor time:

  • Floating with drinks in the water
  • Paddleboarding around the yacht
  • Hanging on the lily pad with music going
  • Taking photos out on the bay
  • Watching the sunset from the water

It feels private, relaxed, and completely different from a crowded public cruise.

Yacht Rental in San Diego

San Diego Bay is one of the most beautiful stretches of navigable water in California. A private yacht rental on Malarky means you get all of it — the Coronado Bridge, the USS Midway, Point Loma at the horizon, and whatever the bay delivers that day — without sharing the deck with strangers.

What makes a San Diego yacht rental different

San Diego Bay is calm, sheltered, and warm — ideal for yacht charters from early spring through late fall and genuinely enjoyable year-round. The bay sits between downtown San Diego and Coronado Island, giving you natural scenery, naval history, and city skyline all in the same frame.

Malarky is a 47-foot Voyage catamaran — twin hulls provide the kind of stability that makes even first-time sailors comfortable, and there’s genuine space to move around: a bow trampoline for lounging close to the water, a shaded aft deck for dining and toasting, and a modern indoor lounge with a mini fridge when you want air conditioning and shade.

Private, not shared

Every Malarky charter is fully private. You’re not on a party boat with other groups; the entire 47 feet belongs to you and your party. Bring your own music playlist, bring your own menu, set the pace you want. Your USCG-licensed captain and crew take care of the navigation; you take care of everything else.

Departures are from America’s Cup Harbor at Shelter Island — one of the better stretches of the bay, with easy parking, a genuine maritime atmosphere, and Ketch Kitchen just steps from the dock if you want a pre-charter bite.

Popular add-ons

Paddleboards, lily pads, kayaks, tower pads, and a waterslide are available to turn a bay cruise into a full day of water activities. These work best when you anchor in a calm spot — ask your captain about the best anchoring locations for the season.

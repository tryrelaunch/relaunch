Anniversary Yacht Charters in San Diego

Some anniversaries deserve more than dinner. A private sunset sail on San Diego Bay — the city glowing behind you, the water catching the last of the light — is the kind of evening that holds up against any memory.

The romantic case for a yacht charter

San Diego Bay at golden hour is the setting. The Coronado Bridge catches the light. Point Loma glows. The city starts to light up behind the mast as the sun drops. This is the backdrop that requires no decoration — the bay does everything you’d spend weeks planning at a venue.

Malarky is private: just you (and whoever you bring along). No other couples on the deck, no restaurant noise, no neighbors at the next table. You control the pace, the playlist, and the menu. The captain handles everything else.

How to book an anniversary charter

Most couples book a 2.5 to 3-hour evening charter timed to catch the sunset — enough time to get out on the water, find a scenic anchor spot, and cruise back in the quiet blue-hour afterward. A bottle of champagne, a meal you brought aboard, and San Diego Bay lit gold: simple and genuinely special.

If you want to mark the occasion with others, Malarky holds up to 12 guests — enough for a small group celebration, family dinner on the water, or a friends-and-family anniversary party.

What to bring

Champagne or wine (white or rosé, no red — no glass cups, plastic or cans only). A meal — something easy to eat on a moving boat works well: charcuterie, nice takeout, restaurant dishes transferred to containers. Flowers are welcome. Decorations are welcome. The crew provides ice and a cooler.

Sailing Catamaran

Sailboat Charters in San Diego — Performance Catamaran

Malarky is a 47-foot Voyage performance catamaran — not a motorboat with a sail strapped to it. When the wind is right, you feel what sailing is supposed to feel like: quiet propulsion, heeling slightly, the city skyline moving past at the pace of the wind.

What makes a catamaran different

A catamaran’s twin-hull design creates fundamentally more stability than a monohull sailboat. On Malarky, that means guests who’ve never sailed before aren’t white-knuckling the rails — the boat stays level enough to walk around comfortably, eat and drink without incident, and genuinely relax.

The Voyage 47 is a blue water catamaran — built for open-ocean passages — which on San Diego Bay translates to stability that’s almost excessive in the best possible way. The hull design also creates generous interior volume: 4 bedrooms, 2 bathrooms, and a full indoor lounge that doesn’t feel like a cave.

When the wind cooperates

San Diego Bay gets consistent westerly winds in the afternoon and evening, especially in summer. When conditions are right, your captain will raise the sails and cut the engine — and that’s the moment most guests realize why people sail. The quiet, the speed, the sense of the boat responding to something invisible. It doesn’t last the whole charter necessarily, but when it happens it’s the part people remember.

San Diego vs. Mission Bay

Malarky charters on San Diego Bay, not Mission Bay. San Diego Bay is larger, more dramatic, and flanked by the downtown skyline on one side and Coronado Island on the other. Mission Bay is pleasant but enclosed; San Diego Bay has sight lines to Point Loma and open-water feel. For a sailing experience with genuine scenery, San Diego Bay is the right venue.

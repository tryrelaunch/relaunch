Anchor and Play

The difference water toys make.

Most San Diego yacht charters are scenic cruises — the boat moves, the skyline scrolls past, the captain narrates landmarks, you sip your drink. Good for a particular kind of afternoon. Limited as a full-day event.

A charter with water toys is a different product. The route stops being the point; the boat becomes a base of operations. Drop anchor in a sheltered cove off Coronado, off Glorietta Bay, or behind Shelter Island, and the next two hours are swim time, slide time, paddleboard time, lily-pad lounging time, lunch on the aft deck, photos on the bow, jump from the tower pad, climb back aboard for another round. The cruise was the way you got to the spot. The water toys are the spot.

The difference shows up most clearly with two groups: families with kids (the slide and the lily pad keep everyone occupied for hours; the cruise alone doesn't) and bachelorette/birthday parties (the waterslide is the photo of the day, the lily pad is the group shot, the kayaks are the wildcard activity for whoever's feeling adventurous). For these groups, water toys turn a charter from a nice afternoon into a memorable event.

When water toys aren't the right call. Sunset charters in cooler months, formal corporate entertaining, anniversary dinners where you want to actually talk — all are best without toys. The cruise itself is the experience. We don't push water toys when the charter doesn't call for them; the captain and crew will recommend if a particular booking does or doesn't make sense.

Malarky is one of the few private yacht charter options in San Diego that offers all five toys as a standard add-on lineup — rather than one or two as an afterthought.

The Signature Add-On

The 11-foot waterslide.

The waterslide is the signature add-on and the most-requested toy on Malarky. Eleven feet of inflatable slide that mounts on the aft deck and drops straight into the water. It deploys at anchor — once the captain has set the anchor and the crew has confirmed conditions, the slide goes up in about ten minutes and stays up for as long as the group wants to use it.

Adults use it constantly. Kids age five and up can use it with a lifejacket. The captain or crew supervises every slide session — not because anyone needs much supervision, but because we want eyes on the entry-into-the-water moment and we want to make sure nobody slides into another swimmer. It's the rhythm of the activity, not a hovering safety presence.

If you've seen a yacht-charter Instagram reel from San Diego in the last few years, there's a decent chance you've seen Malarky's slide. It is the social-media moment of most charters. Bachelorette parties book Malarky for the slide specifically. Birthday groups plan their photographer's shot list around it. Families fight over who gets to go first.

The slide doesn't deploy when conditions are wrong — too much wind, too much swell, too cold for swimming, too much boat traffic where we're anchored. The captain has the final call. On most warm-weather days when the bay is calm (which is most days), the slide goes up and stays up. It's the most reliable conversion-to-fun any of the toys offer.

Stand-Up Paddleboards

Paddleboards for the calm-water lap.

Stand-up paddleboards are the second-most-used toy on Malarky. We carry several boards in adult and youth sizes — enough for a group of four to six guests to be on the water simultaneously. Boards rotate easily between guests, so over a typical anchor stop most of the party will get a turn.

Most guests have never tried paddleboarding before, and that's fine. The crew gives a two-minute how-to dockside or at anchor: how to stand up, how to balance, how to paddle, what to do if you fall in. Total prep time under five minutes. San Diego Bay at anchor is among the easiest places in the country to learn — the water is calm, the boat is right there if you need to climb back aboard, and there's no boat traffic at sheltered anchorages.

Paddleboards are best used at anchor, not while the boat is moving. Guests typically paddle a perimeter around the catamaran, head to a nearby sheltered shore, or just float on the board for the view. Basic swimming ability and a lifejacket are required; the lifejacket is provided.

The Lily Pad

The 15-foot lily pad.

The lily pad is a 15-foot inflatable floating mat that tethers behind the boat at anchor. It holds about six adults comfortably or eight if everyone's willing to lie close. From there it works as four different things at once: a lounge spot in the sun, a jumping platform into the water, a kids' playground for the group's younger members, and a photo backdrop for everyone else.

The lily pad sees the most consistent use of any toy because it doesn't require any skill or supervision — you just climb on. It tends to be the gravity center of the day on family charters and bachelorette outings, with guests rotating on and off as the mood goes. Group photos taken from the boat looking back at the lily pad are some of the best documentation of a charter day.

Best at anchor in calm water. The lily pad isn't deployed when the swell is high or wind is up — same captain's-call as the slide.

Kayaks

Sit-on-top kayaks for the explorers.

For guests who want to actually go somewhere during the anchor stop, we carry single and tandem sit-on-top kayaks. Sit-on-tops (rather than sit-inside) are the right boat for charter use: stable, beginner-friendly, easy to get on and off, and nobody worries about getting trapped if they roll. Two minutes of instruction and any first-timer can paddle.

Common kayak missions: paddle into the shallower coves near anchor (the boat can't follow into shallow water; the kayak can), get the angle on the catamaran from the water for a different photo perspective, explore the mangrove edges at Sweetwater on the rare charters that anchor near there. For active groups, two or three guests will spend most of the anchor stop on kayaks and come back with the best photos of the day.

Beginner-friendly, no experience needed. Lifejackets provided and required.

Tower Pads

Tower pads for the divers.

The tower pads are padded platforms mounted on the boat's rigging at two different heights. They're a jumping/diving platform for guests who want to go in from height rather than off the swim ladder or the slide. The lower platform is the more popular one and is supervised for guests of all ages comfortable with the height. The higher platform is adult-only and requires a brief safety walkthrough with the crew before use.

Tower pads aren't for everyone — some guests get to the platform and decide they're happier with the slide. That's fine. For the divers in your group, the tower pads add a height option that no other rental yacht in the family offers.

How to Add Water Toys

Adding water toys to your charter.

Water toys are optional add-ons on Malarky charters, priced separately per toy. Mention which toys you want when you book and we'll send pricing with your quote. Bundle pricing is sometimes available depending on the charter; ask. See pricing details for the broader charter structure (hourly blocks, seasonal variance), and consider bringing your own drinks to keep the day's total cost reasonable while still going premium on the experience.

Recommended toy combos by group type.

  • Family day: slide + paddleboards + lily pad. The slide and lily pad keep kids occupied; paddleboards give the adults a calm-water activity.
  • Bachelorette party: slide + lily pad. The slide is the photo of the day; the lily pad is the lounge spot between rounds.
  • Kids' birthday party: slide + lily pad + paddleboards. Same logic as family day; the slide is the gravity center.
  • Corporate retreat / team off-site: paddleboards + kayaks for the active types, lily pad for the lounge crew. Most teams split into both groups within an hour of anchor.
  • Mixed-age extended family: all five. Each guest finds their toy.

Lead time and same-day requests.

Mention water toys when you book if you can — we keep the toys in storage when not in use and pulling them out and inflating takes time. Same-day toy requests aren't always possible, especially the lily pad and slide which need 10–20 minutes to inflate. The earlier in the booking process you confirm, the cleaner the day runs.

Where the Water Toys Live

Best anchor spots in San Diego Bay.

Water toys deploy at anchor, not under way. The captain picks the anchorage based on the day's conditions — wind direction, tide stage, water temperature, boat traffic, time of day. Three anchorages get the most use across the season:

Coronado Cays area. Sheltered, sandy bottom, swimmable, easy access from the dock. The default choice for most family and bachelorette charters. Roughly 30 minutes' cruise from the Shelter Island dock.

Glorietta Bay. North of Coronado, behind the Coronado peninsula. Quieter than the Cays, often less traffic, with the Hotel del Coronado in the photo background. Slightly longer cruise — 35 minutes — but the visual payoff is real.

Behind Shelter Island. Closer to the dock (~15 minutes), useful for shorter charters or groups who want to maximize toy time over cruise time. Less visually dramatic than the Coronado spots but very reliable for calm water.

You can request a specific anchorage when you book. The captain reads the day on the morning of the charter and makes the final call on what's safe and feasible. If conditions don't favor your preferred spot, the captain will suggest the next-best option and explain why.

Safety

Safety — what the captain decides.

The captain has the final call on whether each water toy deploys on a given day. Conditions that affect the call: wind speed and direction, swell or chop in the anchorage, water temperature, boat traffic in the vicinity, time of day (later afternoon usually means more wind), and the makeup of the group (kids on board changes some thresholds).

Lifejackets are required for kids of any age and for non-swimming adults on every toy. We provide them, sized for everyone. Even strong swimmers wear them on the lily pad if kids are also using it.

Slide and tower pads are crew-supervised. A crew member is at the entry-into-the-water position for each slide session and at the platform for each tower-pad jump. This isn't intrusive supervision — it's the rhythm of the activity.

No glass and no drinks on the toys themselves. If you've brought drinks aboard (BYOB welcome — we're one of the only yacht charters in San Diego that allows it), set them down on the boat before you slide, jump, or paddle.

Toys retrieve before the boat moves. Lily pad gets pulled aboard, kayaks and boards get reloaded onto the boat, slide deflates and stows. The captain confirms everything's clear before raising the anchor.

Default Inclusions

What's included in every charter.

The charter rate covers the boat being operated by trained professionals for the duration you book, not an à-la-carte buffet you assemble item by item. Every Malarky charter includes:

  • Full private use of the 47-foot Voyage performance catamaran — all three lounge areas (indoor salon, aft deck cockpit, forward bow trampoline), both bathrooms, the upper helm deck.
  • USCG-licensed captain for the full charter duration. Captain handles boat operation, routing, anchor positioning for sunset photos, weather decisions.
  • Crew member who helps with pouring, plating, serving, and general boat operations throughout the charter. Not a waiter — more of a discreet operations hand who's there when you need help and out of frame when you don't.
  • Fuel for standard bay routes (no fuel surcharge for normal anchoring patterns).
  • Ice and a cooler set aside for your beverages.
  • Glassware on request — mention what you need at booking (champagne flutes, wine glasses, lowball, highball).
  • Two onboard bathrooms — standard yacht heads, fully functional, no awkward dock returns mid-charter.
  • Indoor air-conditioned lounge — the rain/heat option, also works for breakout conversations or buffet setup during longer charters.
  • Sound system with Bluetooth audio — bring your playlist on your phone, we handle the connection.
  • Sunset positioning by the captain — if you've booked an evening charter, we position the boat for the best golden-hour photo angle (Coronado Bridge in frame, downtown skyline behind, etc.) without you having to ask.

There is no mandatory catering package built into the rate. No per-person drink minimum. No bar tab. No gratuity automatically added. The number we quote you covers the boat operation; everything else is your call.

Honest Add-Ons

What's not included (honest list).

The transparency section. We want you to know the actual extent of the charter rate before you ask, so the quote doesn't surprise you in either direction. What the rate does not cover:

Food and beverages. BYO is the model — you bring what you want to eat and drink, we provide ice and a cooler. This is a feature, not an oversight. Charter bars typically mark up drinks 3-4x retail; bringing your own bottles is one of the structural cost advantages of Malarky vs charters with built-in bar service. See Section 5 for the full breakdown.

Catering coordination — we don't charge a fee, but the catering itself is your spend. If you want a catered meal aboard, we share a short list of San Diego caterers who know the boat and the dock. You contract directly with them; their fee is separate from your charter rate.

Photographer aboard. Hiring a photographer for proposals, anniversaries, or executive events is your call. We can recommend local pros who've shot many Malarky charters and know the boat — ask at booking. Photographer fees are not built into the charter rate.

Gratuity. Captain-and-crew gratuity is appreciated but not built into the rate. Standard range on charters is 15-20% of the charter rate, similar to a restaurant tip. Some bookings handle this in cash at the end of the charter; some add it to the booking total via the booking system; both are fine.

Decor. Bring your own (flowers, banners, candles when wind allows). We provide vases on request. Nothing else built into the rate.

Water toys and add-ons. Waterslide, lily pad, kayaks, paddleboards, tower pads — available as add-ons, priced separately, varies by season. See Section 6 or the boat rental with water toys page for the full list.

Rate Structure

How charter rates are built.

Five factors determine your quote. We don't list specific dollars here because the actual combination produces a different number for almost every booking, and listing ranges that cover every scenario tends to either overstate or understate the quote a specific customer will actually receive. The factors:

1. Duration. The 2-hour minimum gets you out on the bay and back, which works for a quick sunset cruise but is tight for anything involving anchoring or add-ons. 3 to 4 hours is the standard window for most charter occasions (weddings, birthdays, anniversaries, proposals) — enough time to depart, position for golden hour, anchor briefly, cruise back. 5+ hours is the right window for executive offsites, water-toy days, or multi-occasion bookings. Rates scale roughly linearly through 4 hours, then the per-hour rate flattens for longer blocks.

2. Day of week. Monday through Thursday is the value window — rates are meaningfully lower, and availability is broader. Friday, Saturday, and Sunday carry the weekend premium. The largest premium attaches to Saturday evenings in peak season; the smallest to Friday afternoons in the shoulder season. Sunday mid-day and evening slots in summer are typically the second-most-premium block. If your date is flexible and budget matters, midweek is the lane.

3. Season. May through October is the peak window — warm, dry, long-light evenings, and the highest demand. November through April is shoulder/value — the bay is still beautiful, the bridge still frames the sunset, but the demand profile is meaningfully softer and the rates reflect it. Some of the cleanest sailing days of the year are November and February afternoons; the only thing missing is the warmth, which is a non-issue for most charter use cases (and the indoor lounge handles the rest).

4. Peak-hour positioning. Sunset slots are the single most-requested block, every day of the year. Rates reflect that. A 3pm-6pm charter and a 5pm-8pm charter on the same day of the same week will not be the same rate — the second one captures golden hour and prices accordingly. Mid-day charters (10am-1pm, noon-3pm) are the structural value window within any day, and many groups (water-toy days especially) get better value there.

5. Add-ons. Water toys, photographer coordination, custom catering setup, extended duration past your booked block. All optional, all itemized in the quote, all variable by date.

Once we know your date, duration, and any add-ons, the rate is straightforward to quote. Send us the inputs; we'll send the number.

BYOB Savings

The BYOB cost-savings story.

This is the part of pricing that most charter comparison pages skip. Traditional charters that bundle bar service into the rate are quietly running a 3-to-4x markup on every drink poured. On a premium-bottle evening, that markup is the difference between Malarky pricing and a comparable competitor pricing — before you've even looked at the charter rate itself.

Worked example, two-person anniversary charter, one premium bottle. A bottle of Veuve Clicquot champagne at retail (Costco, BevMo, Total Wine) runs roughly the price of a nice restaurant entrée. The same bottle on a bar-tab charter, with markup and service, runs the price of a full dinner-for-two. On a single bottle, that's a difference of well over a hundred dollars. BYOB on Malarky means you buy the bottle at retail and bring it aboard — the savings on that one bottle alone start to approach the cost of the charter add-on most couples actually want anyway.

Worked example, eight-guest dinner charter, full beverage setup. Two bottles of premium champagne, a bottle of celebration-grade still wine, and a craft-spirits cocktail moment for a toast. At retail: a manageable expense, the kind you'd spend on a nice dinner-out for the group anyway. At bar-tab charter pricing: roughly two-to-three times that, before tips. On an evening like this, BYOB savings clear several hundred dollars compared to the equivalent bar-tab charter. That's the difference between staying in budget or going over — or between the photographer being affordable and the photographer being skipped.

The headline: your charter rate is the charter; your bar budget is yours. No service markup. No drink minimum. No mandatory bottle ordering. Bring what you'd actually want to drink at retail and the math works in your favor. For the full BYOB breakdown including the bottle-rules (no glass at boarding, no red wine on deck, otherwise wide-open), see the BYOB charter page.

Deposit & Cancellation

Deposit and cancellation policy.

Booking requires a deposit at the time of confirmation. The deposit amount is specified in your quote — it varies by charter size and date proximity but is always called out clearly before you commit.

Cancellation policy — 2026 bookings.

2026 Bookings: Private Charters have a 3-week cancellation policy. To receive a full refund of your deposit you must cancel your reservation 3 weeks prior to the event. If canceled within the 3 weeks you will forfeit your deposit. You have the option to reschedule at no charge up until 1 week prior to departure. Your reservation is not eligible for a refund 1 week prior to your event.

Weather flexibility.

If conditions genuinely impair the day — small craft advisory, dangerous wind, fog impairing the bridge backdrop on a sunset charter — the captain calls it and we move you to the next reasonable window at no charge. "Genuinely impair" is the operative phrase: we don't cancel for a few clouds, but we also don't push out into actually-bad conditions to capture the deposit. The captain has the final call.

Corporate invoicing.

For executive events and corporate bookings, we accept purchase orders, advance invoicing, and net-30 terms for established accounts. Full corporate-billing setup is covered on the executive yacht charter page. Email info@malarkycharters.com with your PO details and we'll process within 24 business hours.

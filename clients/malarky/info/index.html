Category 2

The boat: specs, capacity & accessibility.

What you're actually chartering. For the broader product context, see the private yacht charter and luxury yacht rental pages.

A 47-foot Voyage performance sailing catamaran, built in Cape Town, South Africa. Two hulls, four staterooms below deck, two heads (bathrooms), an indoor air-conditioned salon with bar-height counters, an aft deck cockpit with seating for 8 at the table, and a forward bow trampoline. USCG-certified for up to 12 guests on private charters.
Twelve maximum (USCG cap). Most charters book in two scales: couples-only or 2-4 guests for the privacy-oriented bookings, or 6-12 guests for milestone events, executive groups, weddings, bachelorettes, and similar. Twelve is a hard cap; for larger groups, our sister vessel Alana Yacht Rental handles 13-35, and Triton Charters handles 36-100.
Malarky's positioning is adult charters — milestone birthdays, anniversaries, executive events, weddings, bachelorettes, proposals. Kids are not forbidden, but the boat isn't built or staffed for kid-party use cases. Family charters where one or two minors are part of an otherwise-adult group are fine; charters built around a kids' birthday or kid-day-out are a poor fit. For family-with-kids day cruises specifically, our sister vessel Athena Boat Rental is a better fit.
The aft deck cockpit and the indoor lounge are accessible from the dock with a step-up (we can deploy a small portable step or ramp on request). Going below to the staterooms or heads requires stairs that don't accommodate wheelchairs or limited-mobility guests. The aft deck head (in the cockpit) is the primary bathroom for most charters and is accessible from the main deck. Mention any mobility considerations at booking and we'll talk through specifics.

Wide-open BYOB. Champagne, white wine, rosé, sparkling wine, beer, spirits, cocktails, non-alcoholic — all welcome. Two rules: no glass containers at boarding (we transfer wine and champagne to plastic at the dock for safety), and no red wine on deck (it permanently stains the gelcoat; we don't make exceptions). We provide ice, a cooler, and glassware on request.
Yes — fully welcome. Bring whatever you'd want to eat. Charcuterie, sandwiches, a catered spread, full multi-course meals from a partner caterer. We provide trays, plates, and utensils on request. The cockpit table seats 8; the indoor lounge has bar-height counters that work for buffet setup.
Yes. We have a short list of partner caterers familiar with the Shelter Island dock and the boat — mention catering interest at booking and we'll share contacts. Caterers deliver to the marina 30 minutes before departure; we transfer everything aboard. We don't charge a coordination fee; the catering itself is contracted directly between you and the caterer.
Absolutely. Bring whatever cake the occasion calls for; we provide a tray and storage in the mini fridge until it's time. The aft deck cockpit table is the typical cake-cutting setup; for larger group photos, the indoor lounge counter works well.

Captain, crew & discretion.

Who runs the boat and what they actually do. Discretion-trained for proposal, executive, and similar private bookings — see the executive yacht charter page for the full NDA-ready context.

No — the captain is hired separately. The fee is $50 per hour, paid directly to your captain via Apple Pay, Venmo, PayPal, or cash on arrival. We have a roster of USCG-licensed captains for you to choose from, so we'll match you with one that fits your group, the occasion, and the day. You don't need a license or any boating experience yourself.
Yes. We have several USCG-licensed captains who run Malarky regularly. Tell us about your charter when you book — group size, occasion, vibe, any special asks — and we'll match you with the captain who fits best. If you've sailed with one of our captains before and want them again, just say their name.
USCG-licensed, with hundreds of charter days on Malarky specifically. The captain handles boat operation, routing, anchor positioning for sunset photos, weather decisions, and any operational coordination throughout your charter. For executive bookings, the captain signs NDAs on request.
The crew (typically one crew member in addition to the captain) helps with pouring, plating, serving, and general boat operations during the charter. Not a waiter — more of a discreet operations hand who's there when you need help and out of frame when you don't. The crew is discretion-trained for proposal, executive, and similar private bookings: they don't acknowledge what they overhear, don't comment on the guest list, and don't post booking content to social media.
The $50/hr captain fee is the captain's compensation — a tip on top is appreciated but not expected. For the crew, gratuity is also appreciated but not built into the charter rate. A common range is 15-20% of the charter rate (similar to a restaurant tip), handled in cash at the end of the charter or added to the booking total. Discretionary, not required.
Category 6

Water toys & add-ons.

The in-water add-on lineup. For the full breakdown including the bundle logic, see the boat rental with water toys page.

Five categories: an 11-foot waterslide that deploys off the side of the boat, a 15-foot lily pad (floating platform that anchors alongside), single and tandem kayaks, stand-up paddleboards, and tower pads for diving. All available as add-ons priced separately and quoted at booking. Bundling multiple toys for a single charter is meaningfully cheaper per-toy than adding them individually.
The waterslide and tower pads have minimum height/swimming-skill requirements typical of any pool slide — kids who can swim independently are fine; very young kids should stay on the lily pad or paddleboards under supervision. We provide life vests in adult and child sizes. Mention guest ages at booking and we'll guide on which toys work best for the group.
Wind chop above moderate (small craft advisory level) makes the slide and tower pads unsafe to deploy — we'll typically swap to lily-pad-only or skip the in-water portion entirely. Mid-day is the best window for water toys (water warmest, light best, group in actual swim mode). Sunset and after-dark charters typically skip water toys; the slide doesn't work after dark for safety reasons.
Category 7

Weather, parking & logistics.

The operational details for the day-of. For specific questions not covered here, contact us.

Light drizzle or overcast usually proceeds — San Diego rain is typically brief and the indoor lounge is fully air-conditioned with the rain-friendly option. Heavy sustained rain, small craft advisories, dangerous wind, or fog impairing the bridge backdrop on a sunset charter — the captain calls it and we move you to the next reasonable window at no charge. "Genuinely impair" is the threshold; we don't cancel for a few clouds.
Free guest parking at the Shelter Island marina, 2700 Shelter Island Dr, San Diego 92106. Adjacent to the dock. Black-car drop-off and pickup are straightforward; the dock is clearly marked. We send detailed dock instructions in your booking confirmation.
The dock is on the back side of the marina behind Ketch Kitchen and Taps restaurant. Walk through the marina parking lot toward the water; you'll see the slip clearly marked. If you arrive 15 minutes before your charter, the crew will be on the dock and visible from the parking lot.
Fifteen minutes before your scheduled charter departure time. Earlier than that and the boat may not be ready for boarding; later than that compresses your charter time. The captain departs on schedule because charter windows are timed to specific light (golden hour, anchor windows). If you're running late, call (844) 724-5787 from the road so we can adjust expectations.
Layers. San Diego bay in the afternoon is warm; the same bay after sunset is meaningfully cooler with the breeze. A light jacket or sweater is never a bad call for evening charters. Soft-soled shoes (boat shoes, sneakers with light soles) or barefoot — no black-soled shoes that mark the deck. Sunscreen is essential even on overcast days; the water reflects UV. Sunglasses.
Photographers aboard are welcome (mention at booking — they board as one of your guests). Drone photography in San Diego Bay requires proper permits; if you want a drone aerial of the charter, ask in advance and we'll advise on permit logistics. Cell signal across the bay is reliable, so video calls and live streams work without issue if that's part of your charter (executive presentations, virtual surprise reveals, etc.).

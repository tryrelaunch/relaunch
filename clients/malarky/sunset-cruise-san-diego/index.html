Golden Hour on the Bay

Sunset Cruise in San Diego

San Diego sunsets deserve to be seen from the water. The hour before the sun drops behind Point Loma, the bay catches every color — amber, pink, and eventually the deep teal that fades into evening. Malarky’s sunset cruises are timed to catch it all.

Check Availability →Ask a Question

The best seat for a San Diego sunset

The Coronado Bridge frames the western sky like it was designed for sunset photography — and from Malarky’s deck, the angle is exactly right. Point Loma drops into the Pacific at the edge of your view while the downtown skyline starts to light up behind you. It’s a view that doesn’t photograph the way it feels, which is why people keep booking it.

Sunset charters typically run 2.5 to 3 hours — enough time to get underway, settle in with a drink, catch the full color show, and cruise back in the blue-hour quiet afterward. If you’re celebrating something specific (a proposal, an anniversary, a birthday that deserves a sky like that), this is the charter to book.

Golden hour photography

The combination of the boat, the bay, and a San Diego sunset creates genuinely exceptional photos. The backdrop is doing the work — you just need to be on the deck. If you’re bringing a photographer or planning to do the honors yourself, an elevated view from the helm area gives you the full Coronado Bridge-to-skyline panorama. Your crew knows the best spots and moments.

Timing your charter

Departure time depends on the season — sunset shifts from around 5pm in winter to nearly 8pm in summer. We can help you time your departure to catch the best light. Generally, departing 2-2.5 hours before sunset means you arrive at the most scenic stretch of the bay as the light peaks.

Sunset cruises are the most-booked charter type on Malarky. Weekend evenings book earliest — if you have a specific date in mind, check availability early. See also: romantic charters, proposal charters, and anniversary charters.

Why the Disco Boat

The bachelorette boat in San Diego.

The Disco Boat is one of two bachelorette charters in San Diego built around the actual party — not a yacht with a bar added, not a Duffy with a Bluetooth speaker. Every detail of the boat was specced for groups who want to dance.

The interior is the photo set. Mirrored ceiling panels, leopard-print walls, black-and-white checkerboard flooring, a chrome dance pole at center, and a 5-seat rainbow bar with jewel-tone stools. The decor is built in. You don't bring banners and balloons to do the work — the boat already does it.

The rooftop is the golden-hour shot. Turf-padded deck with bean-bag seating, a small rooftop bartop, and a full-size mirrored disco ball that lights up as the sun drops. Coronado in the background, downtown skyline behind that. The group-photo with the bride under the disco ball is the shot most bachelorettes book for.

The captain drives. You party. The Disco Boat is a captained vessel — you add a Disco Boat captain at $50/hour when you reserve, and they handle the helm, the routing, and the docking for the entire charter. Nobody in your group has to be the designated driver.

BYOB welcome. Bring your shaker, your mixers, your champagne for the bride, your "team bride" napkins. We provide the cooler with ice. AirPlay/Bluetooth to the boat speakers for your playlist.

What's included

What a bachelorette charter on the Disco Boat looks like.

A 2–3 hour private charter on San Diego Bay. The boat, the captain, and every space on the boat — interior lounge, rooftop deck, rainbow bar — are yours for the duration.

The boat — private. No shared cruises, no other groups. Just your bachelorette group for the full charter window. Up to 12 guests including the bride.

The captain — $50/hour add-on. Required on every charter, added at booking. Handles routing, docking, safety briefing, and on-water support so nobody in your group has to be the designated driver. Stays out of the photos.

The bar setup. Custom 5-seat rainbow bar with jewel-tone stools. Cooler with ice provided. You bring the bottles, mixers, garnishes, glassware. The bar itself is the decor — nothing to bring.

The music. AirPlay and Bluetooth to the boat speakers. Plug in your bachelorette playlist when you board. Or add the curated-playlist add-on and we'll cue it.

The dock + parking. Shelter Island Drive in Point Loma. Full instructions sent with your booking confirmation — short drive from downtown, the Gaslamp, Coronado, and most San Diego hotels.

Bachelorette FAQ

Bachelorette charter questions, answered.

What's the best bachelorette party boat in San Diego?
The Disco Boat — a 32-foot Suntracker Party Cruiser built as a 1970s disco lounge. Chrome dance pole, rainbow 5-seat bar, mirrored ceiling, leopard-print walls, fold-down windows opening to San Diego Bay, and a rooftop deck with a full-size mirrored disco ball. Up to 12 guests. Departs from Shelter Island in Point Loma.
How many bachelorettes fit?
Up to 12 guests per charter. Bachelorettes of 8–12 are the most common booking — small enough that the bride stays the center of every photo, big enough to feel like a party.
How long is a typical bachelorette charter?
Most bachelorette groups book a 2–3 hour cruise. Late afternoon into golden hour is the most popular window — sunset on the rooftop with the disco ball lit is the moment groups travel for.
Is a captain included?
No. The Disco Boat is a captained vessel, but the captain is a required add-on at $50 per hour, not included in the rental fee. You add the captain when you reserve. No designated driver, no shifts at the helm — the bride and her group stay on the dance floor the whole time.
Can we bring our own drinks?
Yes — BYOB welcome. We provide a cooler with ice. Bring bottles, mixers, garnishes, premade cocktail kits, champagne for the bride. The rainbow bar is yours to set up.
Can we play our own music?
Yes. The boat speakers support AirPlay and Bluetooth. Plug in your bachelorette playlist when you board.
What add-ons should we book?
Most-requested bachelorette add-ons: rooftop photography (group shots ready to post by the time you dock), decorative lighting upgrade (disco-ball uplighting + rainbow accent), and curated playlist. Sashes, banners, and balloons you bring yourself.
Where does the Disco Boat depart from?
2700 Shelter Island Drive, Point Loma, San Diego, CA 92106. On San Diego Bay, a short trip from downtown, the Gaslamp, Coronado, and most San Diego hotels. Dock and parking instructions arrive with your booking confirmation.
When's the best time of day to book?
Late afternoon into sunset. You arrive in daylight for the open-air rooftop, the disco ball lights up as the sun drops, and the golden-hour group photos write themselves. The bay backdrop + mirror-ball sparkle is the shot.

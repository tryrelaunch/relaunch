Peanut Island · Directly Across

Peanut Island, straight off our dock.

The most popular boating destination in the Palm Beach area sits directly across from the Riviera Beach Marina. Designated swimming and snorkeling areas, docks, picnic spots, and a JFK-era presidential bunker, all reachable in your rental within minutes of leaving the dock.

Peanut Island's facilities are second to none: docks, anchorage, designated swimming and snorkeling areas, picnic areas, restrooms, showers, camping, barbeque, and footpaths. The island also features a museum with the JFK presidential nuclear bomb shelter.

Snorkelers can rent premium adult snorkel gear as an add-on when booking any boat, and complimentary dive flags come with every rental. Staff update you on current ocean conditions before you head out.

Want to range further? Munyon Island and Singer Island are popular nearby stops, and the Intracoastal runs north to Jupiter and south past Palm Beach's mansions and mega-yachts.

Dockside Dining · Pull Up & Tie Off

Lunch on the waterway, by boat.

Partner waterfront restaurants with easy boat access line the Intracoastal in both directions: Rafiki Tiki and Sailfish Marina for shorter rentals, and Jupiter's dining scene (Guanabanas, U-Tiki Beach, Square Grouper) when you've booked the faster boats for 4 to 8 hours.

Rather not drive? The Captained Dockside Dining Trip is $450 for up to 4 guests: a private boat and professional Captain, 4 hours from 11:00 am to 3:00 pm, with your restaurant table reserved in advance. You cruise the Intracoastal, circle Peanut Island, and head north toward Jupiter's waterfront dining.

Once docked, you get up to 2 hours for a relaxed lunch. After, your Captain brings you back down the waterway past the mansions and mega-yachts, arriving at the Blue Water dock by 3:00 pm. Food, drinks, and Captain's gratuity aren't included.

Driving yourself? Northbound favorites run from Sailfish Marina minutes away to Lucky Shuks at the Jupiter Inlet. Southbound, E.R. Bradley's in West Palm and Old Key Lime House in Lantana are classics. The full list lives on the Dockside Restaurants page.

Intro to Boating · 3-Hour Lesson

Learn to drive the boat before you rent one.

A 3-hour, hands-on boating lesson with a USCG-licensed Captain: $375 for two students. Basic boat handling, safety procedures, navigation rules, and local waterway knowledge, taught on the water near Peanut Island.

The class starts with safety essentials: required equipment, emergency procedures, weather preparedness, and safe operating practices. Then it's hands-on at the helm: pre-departure checks, starting, steering, throttling, and stopping.

You'll also cover docking and undocking, anchoring technique, and the marine phone apps worth having for navigation and weather, with tips for both gas and electric boats. Each student gets equal time operating the boat.

A minimum of 2 students is required to run each lesson. Bring sunscreen, a hat, and water, dress comfortably, and skip the black-soled shoes. Anyone born on or after January 1, 1988 needs a Boating Safety Education ID Card to operate in Florida, and this course is a strong foundation for passing the test.

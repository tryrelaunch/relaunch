Driving Directions

The drive to Port Clyde.

From the south (about 2 hours from Portland)

  • From the Maine Turnpike northbound, take Exit 44 to Route 295 North.
  • From 295 North, take Exit 28, the Bath/Brunswick Route 1 North exit.
  • Follow Route 1 North to Thomaston, about 60 miles.
  • At the second light in Thomaston, turn right onto Route 131 South (just before the Knox Mansion).
  • Follow Route 131 South 14 miles to Port Clyde, at the end of the peninsula.
  • Look for the Monhegan Boat Line sign at the head of the dock.

From the north (about 2 hours from Bangor)

  • From Bangor, take Route 1A South.
  • Route 1A South merges with Route 1 South; follow it through Rockland.
  • At the first light in Thomaston, turn left onto Route 131 South (just after the Knox Mansion).
  • Follow Route 131 South 14 miles to Port Clyde.
  • Look for the Monhegan Boat Line sign at the head of the dock.

Plan to arrive with time to park and check in — boarding begins 15 minutes before departure, and reservations are required. While you're in the village, the walk out to Marshall Point Light (the "Forrest Gump" lighthouse) is about two miles round trip from the dock.

The Original & Only Year-Round Ferry

Monhegan's connection to the mainland, every day of the year.

Departing from historic Port Clyde, we carry passengers, mail, packages, freight, and fuel across the waters of Muscongus Bay to Monhegan Island, 10 miles off the coast of Maine. Other boats run Monhegan in the summer. We're the only line that runs it all year, and for the people who live on the island, that makes us the lifeline.

  • Year-round, not seasonal. Sun, rain, sleet, or snow, the boat runs, and mail and essentials reach the island 365 days a year.
  • Passengers only, no cars on Monhegan. You ride over, the island stays the quiet place you came to see, and parking at the Port Clyde dock is $10 a day.
  • Two boats with real history. The Laura B has run this coast since the 1940s, and the Elizabeth Ann was purpose-built for the crossing in 1995.
  • Reservations are required, and the crossing fills in summer. Book ahead and arrive 15 minutes before departure.

The Boats · Two 65-Footers

The Laura B, a 1943 working boat that's still on the run.

The world-famous Laura B makes the early-morning trip to Monhegan in summer and delivers all the island's freight year-round. Indoor and outdoor seating, and a history most ferries can't touch.

Built in 1943, the 65-foot Laura B is rigged as a heavy-duty work boat. She was originally a U.S. Army T-57 and spent World War II in the Pacific, serving as a patrol boat carrying troops and supplies. She came under fire in those days, and carried two .50-caliber machine guns on deck.

Brought to Maine in 1946, she spent her early years hauling lobsters from Vinalhaven to Boston and New York City. For the past half century she's ferried passengers, freight, and mail between Port Clyde and Monhegan. A prominent marine surveyor has called her the best-maintained wooden vessel on the Eastern Seaboard.

She's also available for private charters and for hauling freight to any of the islands. The crossing aboard the Laura B runs about 70 minutes each way.

The Boats · Built for the Crossing

The Elizabeth Ann: heated cabin, year-round comfort.

Like the Laura B, the Elizabeth Ann carries passengers and mail to Monhegan year-round. Launched in 1995 and built to the latest marine architecture of her day, she's the comfortable, stable way across the bay.

Sixty-five feet long, the Elizabeth Ann offers seating in a heated, glassed-in cabin, on the covered stern, and up on the sightseeing deck. She has two bathrooms, a real comfort on a crossing in cooler months.

She handles the bulk of the year-round passenger runs and the daily mail. Her crossing time is about 55 minutes each way, the quickest way out to the island.

Whichever boat you ride, you're crossing 10 miles of open water through Muscongus Bay, so dress for the weather on deck, and settle into the cabin if the wind picks up. Boarding begins 15 minutes before departure.

Common charter-website problems

I hear these from boat operators every week. If any sound familiar, I can help.

Your site is slow and dropping bookings

Most charter sites I audit score under 40 on mobile PageSpeed. Tourists on their phones won't wait three seconds. Every slow load is a booking that went to Yelp or your competitor instead.

Your FareHarbor flow is leaking 15–30%

Item structure, add-ons, abandoned cart, mobile checkout. Most operators have never had someone optimize this. I'll show you exactly where the leaks are during the preview.

No landing page for "bachelorette boat party"

Or "wedding charter," or "sunset cruise," or "fishing charter." Tourists search by occasion. If you have one homepage trying to cover everything, you rank for nothing.

Black Friday with no playbook

Most charters wing it or skip the season entirely. The Black Friday system I run did $535K at Triton last year, $96K at Malarky. I'll install yours during the build year-round prep.

Your Google reviews aren't on your site

You've worked years for those stars. They should be on every page with AggregateRating schema so Google shows them in search results — not buried on a separate Yelp link.

Your old site is killing your Google rankings

Missing schema, broken mobile, slow load, no review markup, no landing pages. Your competitors aren't winning because their business is better. Their site is set up better.

The playbook · 3 brands · 9 campaigns · 3 years

$1.53M across three brands. Same playbook. Same FareHarbor account architecture. Same operator.

FareHarbor first wrote about this campaign when it hit $250,000 in six days at Triton. Since then I've run it nine more times across three different marine brands — Triton, Malarky, and Alana. Public-record FareHarbor dashboard data, three full years apiece. $1.53 million in gross revenue. ~14,900 paid orders. 32,900+ future guests booked. Refund rate averaging well under 0.5%.

The growth curves matter as much as the totals. Triton was already mature — the system grew it +53% over three years. Malarky started at $12K and grew to $96K (+714%). Alana started at $15K and grew to $51K (+242%). Three different brands, three different growth profiles, all positive. The playbook isn't just for optimizing mature operations. It builds them from low base.

Operator-level detail: Black Friday day itself does ~36–38% of the entire campaign at Triton. That's not a "Black Friday week" promotion — it's a one-day demand spike with a long tail. The system has to be ready for both.

That's the playbook I install. Not a generic agency package. The actual machine — the landing pages, the FareHarbor offer structure, the email/SMS sequences, the GBP work, the seasonal calendar — adapted to your specific operation.

What gets installed

The system, not just a pretty website.

A new site is the wrapper. What actually moves bookings is the operating system underneath it. Here's what I install on every engagement.

01 · Site

Custom-coded fast website

Built around your actual booking flow — not a template. Hand-coded HTML/CSS, no WordPress bloat, 90+ PageSpeed mobile, schema markup baked in. The booking CTA is the focus of every page.

02 · SEO

Local + intent-based SEO structure

One page per high-intent search term ("private yacht charter [your city]", "sunset cruise [your city]", "[occasion] boat rental [your city]"). LocalBusiness + Service schema. Internal linking. Real-content metadata.

03 · GBP

Google Business Profile rebuild

Audit, rewrite, and optimize your GBP. Categories, services, attributes, photo strategy, Q&A, posts. The single highest-leverage asset most marine operators leave on the table.

04 · Reviews

Review capture system

Post-charter SMS/email flow that asks at the right moment, makes Google the destination, and surfaces unhappy guests privately before they leave a bad public review. This is how you build a review moat.

05 · FareHarbor

FareHarbor flow optimization

I'll audit your existing FareHarbor setup — flows, item structure, add-ons, abandoned-cart, mobile checkout. Most operators leave 15–30% of conversion on the floor here. We close that gap.

06 · Campaigns

Seasonal campaign system

Black Friday, summer kickoff, holidays, off-season retention. Pre-built landing pages, email + SMS sequences, FareHarbor offer structure, ad creative. The exact stack that did $250K → $700K at Triton.

Why generic agencies miss

A pretty boat site and one that gets booked are two different things.

Tourists scan photos, price clues, group size, what's included, where to park, whether they can bring drinks, whether the captain is included, and whether the booking button feels safe. Most agencies build past every one of those signals.

Generic web agency
  • Pretty pages, vague copy
  • Template hero, template sections
  • Contact form as the conversion event
  • Broad "we do SEO" without intent targeting
  • No FareHarbor flow audit — bookings end up worse
  • One-size-fits-all monthly retainer
  • No idea what "Coney pizza" means, or what BYOB rules matter, or why your captain is hired separately

  • Direct booking intent on every page
  • Group size, route, included items, parking — above the fold
  • FareHarbor booking CTA as the primary action

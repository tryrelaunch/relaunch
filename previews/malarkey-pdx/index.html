What is The Malarkey

A little wild, a little glamorous.

The Malarkey is chef Brian Malarkey's homecoming: an Oregon kid back from a thirty-restaurant career in San Diego to build his most ambitious place yet. Eleven thousand square feet of wood fire, raw bar, and themed rooms, dreamed up as a love letter to Portland.

It plays more like a theme park than a dining room: downtown grit, a stage, a forest you can walk into, and a nod to every Portland folk hero along the way. Wander in. Don't expect to leave the same.

Room 01 · The Coast

The Bar & Raw Bar

Pull up to the elk and settle in. The Coast is where the night starts: Pacific Northwest oysters on ice, a tower stacked with crudo and crab, and cocktails that don't take themselves too seriously. There's a stage, a weathered art wall, and the kind of lighting that makes everyone look like they have a secret.

Room 02 · The Heights

The Dining Room

White oak, cane-backed banquettes, and bolsters that double as dividers so every table feels like its own little balcony. This is the room for the long dinner: wood-fired steaks, whole fried rockfish, the Sunday-best version of a Tuesday night. Counter-height seats keep the energy up; the rounded corners keep it soft.

Wood-Fired SteaksWhole FishBig TablesCounter Seats
Room 03 · The Forest

The Forest Rooms

Step into the deep green and the city goes quiet. The Forest Rooms are the hideaway: darker, closer, built for the dinner you'll still be talking about next year. Bring the people you actually like. Order the truffle cavatelli. Stay too long.

Room 04 · Hazel Ying

The Hazel Ying Room

Named for Hazel Ying Lee, the Portland-raised aviator who flew when the world told her not to, and a fitting patron saint for a restaurant that doesn't mind breaking a few rules. The room wears its color loud: original murals, a decorative roofline, and a private table for the celebrations worth closing the doors for.

// MANIFESTO

Portland deserves a restaurant that acts like Portland.

My name is Brian Malarkey. I opened thirty restaurants before this one. I went to San Diego at seventeen and stayed gone for three decades. Then I came home.

This city was decimated after COVID. People stopped showing up downtown. Critics wrote the obituary. Half the operators I respect packed up and left.

So I bet eleven thousand square feet, fifty employees, my reputation, and a number on the door that says we have to do nine million in year one. Cocky? Yes. Arrogant? Probably. The name fits.

If we pull this off, Portland gets the room it's been missing. If we don't, you can say you ate here first.

— Brian Malarkey
// FOUR ROOMS. ONE TICKET.

One restaurant. Four cities.

01
// DOWNTOWN

The Downtown Room

A little bit gritty, the way downtown Portland actually feels. Old brick, graffiti tags, scuffed floors, exposed beams. The dining room that doesn't pretend it's anywhere else. Sit close to the open kitchen and watch the line yell at each other.

02
// STAGE

The DJ Floor

A real stage. A real booth. Real bass. Dinner service does not stop when the music starts — it gets louder. Wednesdays through Sundays, the kitchen plates to the beat.

03
// TRAIN YARD

The Boxcars

Two full-size train cars dropped into the back of the building. Booths inside. Graffiti walls outside. The boxcar doors slide open onto the forest. Best private dining in the city or you eat free.

04
// FOREST

The Magical Forest

Yes, an actual forest. Real trees. Fog. A canopy of lights. Cocktails out here at dusk feel like you snuck out of dinner and into a David Lynch movie. Reservations recommended after 8pm.

// TASTE OF THE OPENING MENU

Chef-driven. Ego-fueled.

Brian Malarkey + Chef Carlos. Pacific Northwest with a chip on its shoulder.

The Malarkey

42

Dry-aged Oregon wagyu, smoked marrow butter, sourdough black pepper crust. The dish the place is named after. Eat it first.

Tumalo Ranch Lamb

38

Lamb from the ranch Brian grew up on in Central Oregon. Charred over juniper. Served with smoked stone-fruit jam and a hard look at his teenage self.

D.B. Cooper Old Fashioned

16

Smoked rye, parachute-grade bitters, a single ice cube the size of a flight log. Garnished with a hundred-dollar bill (chocolate).

Long-Time Romaine

18

Whole charred heart of romaine, cured anchovy, brown butter parmesan crumb. Caesar's louder, meaner cousin.

Boxcar Oysters

28

Half-dozen Oregon coast, mignonette three ways, served on a length of railroad iron. Yes, really.

Forest Floor

22

Wild mushrooms foraged forty miles east of here, smoked yolk, charred bread. Dessert in the back room is on the house if you finish it.

Full menu drops opening week. Tasting flights at the bar.

What to expect

A pizza spot that used to be a Taco Bell.

The drive-thru is gone. The slanted roof and stucco walls are still here. Inside it's tables, booths, ten taps lined up behind the counter, a wall of arcade machines and pinball, and a kitchen that runs the full Detroit-style menu seven days a week.

Order at the counter, grab a number, sit anywhere. We bring the pizza to you. Kids welcome — the arcade keeps them occupied while the pies bake. Adults, you've got beer, sports, and pinball.

It's the daytime, full-menu, family-friendly version. If Point Loma is the nighttime speakeasy, Kearny Mesa is where you bring the family for Sunday lunch — or where you grab a beer after work and accidentally stay for two hours.

Kearny Mesa — questions

The honest answers.

Is it really kid-friendly?
Yes — the arcade alone is worth bringing a kid. We have a kids' menu (Detroit-style cheese slice + side), high chairs, changing tables in the bathrooms, and most weekday afternoons there are families in here.
Can I watch the game?
We've got TVs around the dining room and at the bar. Major NFL, NBA, MLB, and any San Diego team always on. Bring a group — we'll find you a spot with a screen.
Are the arcade games free?
Spend $20+ on food and we'll comp arcade tokens. Otherwise it's a buck a play. Pinball is always a quarter. The high score on the Pac-Man machine has been held by the same regular since 2023.
Do you do delivery?
Kearny Mesa is on DoorDash and Uber Eats. Honestly — Detroit-style is best eaten at the restaurant. The crispy cheese edge softens inside a delivery box. Pickup is the move if you have to take it home.
Where do I park?
Free lot directly in front of the building. Off Overland Ave between Engineer and Convoy. The lot fits about 30 cars — even on a Friday night we usually have space.

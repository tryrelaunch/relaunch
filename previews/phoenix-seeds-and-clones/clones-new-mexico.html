Why we spun up a NM-specific brand.

New Mexico legalized adult-use cannabis in April 2022. Same general framework as Arizona — 6 plants per adult, 12 per household max — but a different geography, a different licensing regime, and a different customer base. Trying to run NM as a satellite of the Phoenix operation never made sense.

We brought in Uncle Smoke, a California cultivator with decades in the dirt, who relocated his family to New Mexico to run the program full-time. He's fully licensed in NM and operates out of an Albuquerque facility — close enough to deliver bulk clones to commercial cultivators across the state, and to ship retail orders priority express to anyone outside the metro.

Same hand-bred standards as Phoenix. Same kind of strains we'd grow ourselves. The only thing that's different is who's running it day to day.

New Mexico cannabis law, in one breath.

Adults 21 and over can grow up to 6 plants per adult, with a 12-plant household maximum. Follow the law — don't double the allowance. With autoflower seeds you can get 5–6 harvests a year, so be patient.

Most NM dispensaries don't sell clones or seeds, because every plant you grow at home is one you don't buy from them. We're not in that business model, so our incentive is to give you the best genetics on earth and keep you coming back season after season.

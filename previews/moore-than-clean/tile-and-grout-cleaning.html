Prineville's Water Is Hard On Tile

If you've lived in Prineville long enough, you already know — our water leaves a mark. Calcium, lime, and mineral deposits build up on tile surfaces and seep into porous grout lines over time. That's why grout that started white turns gray. Why showers develop that stubborn film. Why kitchen tile never quite looks like it did the day it was laid.

Most of it isn't dirt. It's mineral buildup, and store-bought cleaners can't touch it. We use professional pressurized hot water extraction with specialty solutions designed specifically for hard water deposits. The difference is usually visible in the first square foot.

Our Process

1. Inspection. We identify the tile type (ceramic, porcelain, travertine, slate) and grout condition. Different materials call for different methods.

2. Pre-treatment. Specialty pre-spray loosens hard water deposits, soap scum, and biological buildup before the main pass.

3. Pressurized extraction. Hot water under controlled pressure lifts everything out of the grout lines simultaneously while a vacuum head extracts the dirty water — none of it stays on your floor or runs into your drain unfiltered.

4. Optional sealing. Penetrating grout sealer locks out moisture and prevents the cycle from starting over. Strongly recommended for shower and high-moisture floors.

About Mold & Mildew

Mold in shower grout isn't just cosmetic — it's a moisture-and-spore loop that gets worse the longer it sits. We treat with eco-friendly antimicrobial products that kill mold and mildew, then we recommend resealing the grout to keep moisture out. Most clients are surprised how much brighter the bathroom feels afterward.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does hard water affect tile? Mineral buildup discolors grout and films tile surfaces. Pressurized hot water and specialty solutions break it down — store-bought cleaners can't.

Can you remove shower mold? Yes — antimicrobial treatment + grout resealing usually solves it.

Do you seal grout? Yes — penetrating sealer that doesn't change the look. Highly recommended after deep cleaning.

How long does tile cleaning take? Standard bathroom 1–2 hours. Kitchen 2–3 hours. Larger jobs quoted on walkthrough.

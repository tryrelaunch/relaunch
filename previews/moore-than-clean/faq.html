Pricing & Estimates
What does it cost and how do estimates work?
How much does professional carpet cleaning cost in Prineville?

Most homes in Prineville fall in the $150 to $400 range depending on square footage, number of rooms, and the level of soiling. We give every customer a free estimate up front — no surprise charges. Commercial pricing is per square foot and depends on traffic and frequency.

Do you offer free estimates?

Yes — every estimate is free and no-obligation. Most quotes happen over the phone in under 5 minutes. For larger or unusual jobs we may schedule a quick walkthrough.

Are there hidden fees?

No. The price you're quoted is the price you pay. Stain treatment, pre-spray, and standard furniture moving are included. Optional add-ons like fabric protection are quoted separately and only added if you ask for them.

Do you charge by room or by square foot?

Most residential jobs are quoted per room with a discount as room count goes up. Commercial work is per square foot. Either way, you'll know the full price before we start.

Do you offer discounts for repeat or recurring customers?

Yes. Recurring residential and commercial clients get preferred scheduling and locked-in pricing. Just ask when you call.

Cleaning Process & Methods
How we actually clean carpets
What method do you use to clean carpets?

We use truck-mounted hot water extraction (HWE), often called steam cleaning. It's the method recommended by most major carpet manufacturers. Pressurized hot water and eco-friendly cleaning solution are injected into the carpet, then immediately extracted along with the dirt — leaving carpets clean, not soaked.

Is steam cleaning the same as hot water extraction?

Essentially yes. Most professionals use the terms interchangeably. We use truck-mounted hot water extraction — water heated to a high temperature, pressurized, applied with cleaning solution, then vacuumed back out in the same motion.

Do you do dry cleaning instead of steam?

Hot water extraction is our standard method because it gets carpets the cleanest. For specialty fabrics or situations where extreme low-moisture is required, we have alternative methods. Tell us your situation and we'll recommend the right approach.

Why is truck-mounted equipment better than portable?

Truck-mounted units produce hotter water, stronger suction, and more consistent results than portable units. The heat alone makes a big difference for breaking down embedded soil and oils. We use professional truck-mounted equipment on every job.

How long does a typical carpet cleaning take?

Most homes take 1 to 3 hours depending on size, number of rooms, and soiling level. Larger homes or heavily soiled carpets may take longer. We can give you a tighter estimate when you call.

Drying Time & After Care
When can you walk on it again?
How long does it take for carpets to dry?

Carpets are usually walkable within 2 to 4 hours of cleaning. Full drying takes 6 to 12 hours depending on humidity, ventilation, and how heavily soiled the carpet was. Running fans or AC speeds it up.

Can I walk on the carpet right after cleaning?

We recommend waiting at least an hour or two before walking on freshly cleaned carpet, and even then, wear clean socks or shoe covers (we provide them). Avoid moving furniture or playing on the carpet until it's fully dry.

Can I put my furniture back the same day?

Most furniture can go back within 4 to 6 hours, but we recommend waiting until the carpet is fully dry (6 to 12 hours). For wood or metal furniture legs, we'll place protective foam blocks under them to prevent staining or rust marks during drying.

What can I do to speed up drying time?

Run ceiling fans, open windows if the weather allows, turn on the AC, or set up a portable fan in the cleaned area. Dry, moving air is what you want.

Safety: Pets, Kids, & Allergies
Eco-friendly is a non-negotiable for us
Are your cleaning products safe for kids and pets?

Yes — that's the whole point. Moore Than Clean uses eco-friendly products specifically chosen to be tough on dirt but gentle on family and pets. No harsh chemicals, no lingering fumes, no residue. Same standard we use in our own home.

Will the cleaning products affect my allergies?

Most clients with allergies report better air quality after professional cleaning, not worse. Hot water extraction pulls dust mites, pollen, dander, and mold spores out of the carpet — and our products are formulated to leave no chemical residue behind.

Do I need to leave the house during cleaning?

No, you don't have to leave. The products are family- and pet-safe. That said, the equipment is loud (truck-mounted units have an exterior compressor), so many clients prefer to step out or work from a different room while we clean.

Stains & Odors
Wine, pet urine, coffee, and the rest
Can you remove pet urine stains and odors?

Yes. We have a dedicated urine and odor treatment using specialty enzyme-based products. Even old, set-in pet urine usually comes out completely. For severe cases that have soaked into the pad or subfloor, we may recommend additional treatment.

Can you remove old or set-in stains?

Most stains, even old ones, can be significantly improved or removed entirely. Some stains (red wine, certain dyes, bleach damage) are tougher and we'll be honest about expectations during the walkthrough.

What about red wine, coffee, and food stains?

Red wine, coffee, juice, and most food stains respond well to professional pre-treatment + hot water extraction. The faster you call after a spill, the better the outcome — but we've removed plenty of stains that have been there for months.

Can you fix carpet that's been damaged by bleach?

Bleach permanently removes color from carpet fibers — no cleaning method restores it. For bleach damage we'd recommend dye replacement or patching, which is a different specialty. We can refer you to a local pro if needed.

Will stains come back after cleaning (wicking)?

Sometimes a stain that was deep in the carpet pad can wick back to the surface as the carpet dries. We use techniques specifically to prevent this. If a stain does reappear within 30 days, give us a call — we'll come back and re-treat it.

Frequency & Maintenance
How often should you clean?
How often should I have my carpets professionally cleaned?

Most carpet manufacturers recommend professional cleaning every 12 to 18 months to maintain warranties. Households with kids, pets, or allergies often benefit from cleaning every 6 to 9 months. Commercial spaces typically need quarterly or bi-monthly cleaning.

Does professional cleaning extend the life of my carpet?

Yes — significantly. Embedded dirt acts like sandpaper on carpet fibers, slowly cutting them as you walk. Regular professional cleaning removes that grit and can double or triple the lifespan of your carpet.

Should I vacuum before you arrive?

It helps but it's not required. We do a thorough vacuuming as part of our process. If you have time to do a quick once-over before we arrive, it doesn't hurt — but don't stress if you can't.

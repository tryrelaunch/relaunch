The story

From a 1962 Airstream
to one Bend kitchen.

Spork started in 2009 as a globally-inspired streetfood truck, housed in a 1962 Airstream Tradewind that sailed the streets and events of Central Oregon. We were voted Best Chow Cart in Bend every year we ran it.

In June 2013, we put down roots on Newport Avenue. Three of us — chef Jeff Hunt, hospitality maven Erica Reilly, and visionary Chris Lohrey — set out to build a place that wasn't just a restaurant, but a workplace and a watering hole. Four-day workweeks. Health benefits. A percentage of gross sales into a community abundance fund. Pop-ups for up-and-coming Bend chefs.

Jeff's food draws from years of travel — Vietnam, Japan, Spain, Mexico, Peru. Mint, basil, cilantro on every dish. Latin American and Southeast Asian flavors, often in the same bowl. We don't call it fusion. We just cook what we love.

We've always tried to present food in a way that feels like an eye of travel. It's a bit of a watering hole. — Erica Reilly, co-owner

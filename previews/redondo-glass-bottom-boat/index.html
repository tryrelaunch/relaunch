Captain Jake Mark — a South Bay lifer who built the boat himself.

The vessel was built in 1995, annually inspected by the U.S. Coast Guard, and outfitted with an underwater viewing chamber featuring 7-foot by 2-foot windows. Captain Jake grew up here, raised three kids here, and runs every tour with one priority: families having a good time.

  • USCG Licensed Captain. Jake holds a current Coast Guard captain's license and has worked on King Harbor since 2015.
  • Family-first. With three kids of his own, Jake builds every tour around what makes families actually enjoy the water.
  • Annual Coast Guard inspections. The vessel passes USCG inspection every year — safety isn't a marketing line, it's a legal requirement we meet.
  • Featured on KCAL News. The crew + the underwater chamber made local TV.
What to expect on your tour

Two decks. One 45-minute tour. Marine life from above and below.

Every tour follows the same flow — start on the top deck with California Sea Lions, then descend into the viewing chamber to see what's actually underneath you.

01 · TOP DECK
Above-Deck Adventure
We start on the top deck so you can see the California Sea Lions up close, take in the coastal charm of King Harbor, and get oriented before heading below. Bring a camera — the sea lions are not shy.
02 · UNDERWATER CHAMBER
Underwater Exploration
Step down into the viewing chamber with 7-foot by 2-foot windows. Get within inches of the glass (please don't touch — the marine life can feel it). Garibaldi, Calico Bass, Opaleye, and more cruise past.
